Perched atop the rocky peninsula of Cape Fisterra, nearly 140 meters above sea level, the **Fisterra Lighthouse** is undoubtedly one of the most iconic in Europe. Its imposing presence over the **Costa da Morte** (Coast of Death), historically known for its frequent shipwrecks, offers one of the most powerful images of the Atlantic.

Built in 1853, this lighthouse is not only a vital guide for sailors with its 65-kilometer range but also the symbolic final destination for pilgrims on the Way of Saint James. Today, the complex houses an information center and the *O Semáforo* hotel, where guests can sleep to the sound of the ocean's roar and enjoy infinite views from its terraces. It is a place where the land ends and true disconnection begins.
