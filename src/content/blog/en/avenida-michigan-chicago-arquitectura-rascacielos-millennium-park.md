**Michigan Avenue** is Chicago's main artery, and strolling down it feels most like being inside an imposing concrete canyon. It's a place where the eye receives an unstoppable carousel of beautiful and surprising images, housing endless skyscrapers designed by history's most notable architects. The scale of these giants is so colossal that, inevitably, you feel tiny—like an insignificant Gulliver wandering through a land of giants.

To fully enjoy it, one must walk the avenue at different times of day, observing the play of light on glass and steel. An unavoidable stop at sunset is **Millennium Park**, a place where art and architecture merge masterfully: from Frank Gehry's avant-garde Jay Pritzker Pavilion to Anish Kapoor's mystical *Cloud Gate* sculpture and Jaume Plensa's innovative *Crown Fountain*. Chicago is undoubtedly an essential destination for any lover of urban life and travel in its grandest sense.
