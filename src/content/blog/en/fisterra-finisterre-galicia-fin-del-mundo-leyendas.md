Its name derives from the Latin *finis terrae* (land's end), and for centuries, it was considered the literal end of the known world. **Fisterra** is more than just a geographic feature; it is a place charged with mysticism that has captivated travelers and Roman legions alike, who watched in awe as the sun sank into these wild waters.

The uniqueness of Fisterra arises from the countless legends that surround its stones and sea, intertwining religious and maritime themes. As the final destination for many pilgrims continuing their journey after reaching Santiago de Compostela, the cape offers breathtaking views where the sea seems to have no end. Visiting Fisterra, especially after traveling through inland Galicia and the Rías Altas, is to gaze upon that "End of the World" which, despite modern maps, still retains all its legendary and spiritual power.
