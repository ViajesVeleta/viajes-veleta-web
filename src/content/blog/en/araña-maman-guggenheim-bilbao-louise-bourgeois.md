Located outside the **Guggenheim Museum Bilbao**, under the shadow of the Salve Bridge, stands one of the most iconic sculptures in contemporary art: **Maman**. This monumental spider, the work of French artist Louise Bourgeois, stands 9 meters tall and has become an inseparable presence in the Bilbao landscape.

Far from being a threatening figure, the sculpture was conceived by Bourgeois as a profound tribute to her mother, who was a weaver. For the artist, the spider symbolizes intelligence, protection, and the tireless effort of the maternal figure. Crafted in bronze, stainless steel, and marble, its slender silhouette and eight legs invite visitors to walk beneath it, creating a unique interaction between human scale and artistic grandeur. Alongside Frank Gehry's avant-garde building, the spider guards the museum, reminding us that sometimes strength and delicacy weave a city's history together.
