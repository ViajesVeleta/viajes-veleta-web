Located on the outskirts of Amarillo, Texas, **Cadillac Ranch** is one of the most iconic and unique stops on the legendary **Route 66**. This art installation, created in the early 1970s by the Ant Farm collective, features ten Cadillac cars half-buried in the ground.

A curious detail is that the vehicles are buried at exactly the same angle as the pyramids of Giza. Over the decades, what began as an artistic proposal has transformed into a giant interactive canvas where travelers can leave their own spray-painted graffiti. This site is also a pilgrimage point for music lovers, as it has been mentioned in numerous songs, becoming an eternal symbol of freedom and the rebellious spirit of the American road.
