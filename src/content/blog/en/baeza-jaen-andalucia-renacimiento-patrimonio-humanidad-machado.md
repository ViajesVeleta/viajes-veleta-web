The city of **Baeza** sits in the heart of the province of Jaén, overlooking from its hilltop an infinite landscape of olive groves that look like waves of a silver sea. Declared a **World Heritage Site** by UNESCO, Baeza is a historical-artistic ensemble of exceptional richness.

Upon arrival, the city presents itself as quiet and peaceful, with a sobriety that sometimes evokes Castile more than Andalusia. Its beauty lies in the carved stone of its palaces, churches, and manor houses, most belonging to the most splendid period of the Spanish Renaissance. But Baeza is not just architecture; it is inextricably linked to the figure of **Antonio Machado**. The spirit of one of our most universal poets still seems to permeate every square and corner, inviting the traveler to a slow and reflective stroll. Walking through Baeza is, in essence, entering a poem sculpted in stone.
