The **Granada Geopark** stretches across an exceptional territory in eastern Andalusia, encompassing 47 municipalities in the regions of Guadix, Baza, and Huéscar. This landscape, traditionally known as the "hoyas" (basins) of Guadix and Baza, is an open book on geological history over the last five million years.

What we see today as stunning gullies and *badlands* is the result of river erosion over the last half-million years. Previously, for millions of years, the territory was a vast river system with no outlet to the sea, draining into a large lake and accumulating one of Europe's best records of continental Quaternary sediments. The region's semi-arid nature and sparse vegetation allow for breathtakingly clear observation of the earth's horizontal layers, creating an almost lunar landscape that has shaped the lives and culture of its inhabitants from prehistoric times to the present day.
