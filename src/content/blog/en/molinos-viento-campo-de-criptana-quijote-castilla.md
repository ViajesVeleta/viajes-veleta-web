"At this point they came in sight of thirty or forty windmills that there are on that plain...". Thus begins one of the most immortal scenes in world literature, and the place that inspired Miguel de Cervantes is none other than **Campo de Criptana**, in Ciudad Real.

Against these peaceful giants of stone and sail, Don Quixote fought without dismay, confusing them with evil beings in his noble delusion. Today, the Criptana windmills still watch over the Castilian horizon, offering one of Spain's most beautiful and authentic images. Their white facades and conical roofs are a reminder of our history, but also of the magic of words that transformed farming tools into eternal myths. An essential landscape for any lover of travel and literature.
