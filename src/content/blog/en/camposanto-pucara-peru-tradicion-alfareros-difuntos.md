On the occasion of All Saints' Day, we share this photograph of the cemetery in the small city of **Pucará**, Peru. Captured during our journey through the area, the image reflects the sober beauty of this resting place situated in an environment laden with history and tradition.

Pucará is a land of expert potters, world-famous for the **\"Toritos de Pucará\"** (Pucará bulls) that inhabitants place on rooftops as symbols of protection and prosperity. It was also the cradle of an important pre-Inca culture of the same name, known for its pyramids and advanced hydraulic engineering knowledge. From the cemetery to the ceramic workshops, Pucará is a place where clay turns into art and ancestral memory remains alive in every corner, protected by the same bulls that watch over the horizon from the heights of the homes.
