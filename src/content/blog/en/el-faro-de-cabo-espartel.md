The Cape Espartel lighthouse was the first one to illuminate the Moroccan coast. While construction began in 1861, it did not become operational until 1864 at the request of European powers aimed at curbing shipwrecks in the Strait of Gibraltar. A century and a half later, the lighthouse and the city of Tangier remain an essential link between African and European markets.

Located 14 kilometers from central Tangier, Cape Espartel offers one of the most beautiful sunsets right at the point where the two seas merge. Beyond touring the lighthouse, the Cape Nature Reserve features cliffs, beaches and forests ideal for birdwatchers. Just 5 km away lie the Caves of Hercules, seaside grottos sculpted by the wind and sea and steeped in local legend.
