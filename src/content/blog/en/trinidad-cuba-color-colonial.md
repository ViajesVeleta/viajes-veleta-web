Indigo, canary yellow, smurf blue, and bubblegum pink. The facades of **Trinidad** unfold before the eyes like an infinite streamer of colors. Located about 300 km from Havana, this city is a direct trip to the splendor of the Spanish colonial period of the 18th and 19th centuries.

Strolling through its **Plaza Mayor**, under the white of its lampposts and the green of its palm trees, is to immerse oneself in a history marked by the sugar industry. But Trinidad is much more than its museums; it is the music that plays on every step, the proximity of the tropical beaches of **Playa Ancón**, and the untamable spirit of the Escambray mountains. A city where time seems to have stood still on an eternal afternoon of son and salsa.
