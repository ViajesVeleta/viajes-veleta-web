**Charging Bull: The bronze heart that never sleeps**

If you visit New York, there is a must-stop in the Financial District that represents much more than a simple sculpture: the **Charging Bull**. This bronze colossus of more than 3 tons, located in the historic Bowling Green square, is the ultimate symbol of optimism and strength of the American market.

What many don't know is that its origin was an act of rebellion. Artist **Arturo Di Modica** installed it illegally in front of the Stock Exchange in 1989 as a Christmas gift after the financial crisis, seeking to inspire New Yorkers to “charge” against adversity. Today, it is one of the most photographed spots in the world.
