With its ancient cobbled streets, grandiose Baroque churches, and the remains of Inca temples with century-old carvings, no city stands out in Andean history like **Cuzco**. Inhabited since pre-Hispanic times and once the capital of the Inca Empire, this vibrant town is much more than the gateway to Machu Picchu.

Mystical, commercial, and chaotic, Cuzco is a unique city that remains a wonder for the senses. It is the place where you encounter women in traditional attire leading llamas, museums dedicated to magical plants, and the most animated nightlife in the high Andes. As **Lonely Planet** aptly describes, Cuzco is the beating soul of Peru.
