They say of **Santillana del Mar** that it is the "town of the three lies," because it is neither holy (santa), nor flat (llana), nor does it have a sea (mar). However, what it does have is an absolute truth: it is one of Spain's most beautiful and best-preserved towns. Recently included in National Geographic's list of the country's most beautiful villages, Santillana is a direct journey to the heart of mountain and medieval architecture.

The town's core is the **Collegiate Church of Santa Juliana**, a Historic-Artistic Monument since the late 19th century. Around it unfolds a layout of cobblestone streets flanked by blazoned mansions and Baroque palaces. Walking along La Carrera or Del Río streets means enjoying wooden balconies overflowing with geraniums and heraldic shields that narrate the prestige of families who made fortunes in the Americas. Santillana del Mar is not just visited; it is traversed slowly to absorb the legacy of centuries that seem to have stood still among its stone walls.
