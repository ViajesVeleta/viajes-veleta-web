Hidden behind a massive rock wall in the province of Palencia lies the church of **Saints Justo and Pastor in Olleros de Pisuerga**. Defined by scholars as \"the cathedral of Spanish rock-cut architecture,\" this temple is an amazing testimony to the eremitic movement that sought spirituality in the solitude of stone.

Excavated entirely from the rock, its interior surprises by imitating all the elements of a traditional Romanesque temple: two naves, a barrel vault, and apses, all carved directly into the mountain. It is a living parish church that continues to host services, where faith and geology merge into an unforgettable overall effect.
