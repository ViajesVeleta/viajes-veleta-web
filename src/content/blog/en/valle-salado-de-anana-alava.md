Salt exploitation in the **Valle Salado de Añana** is an industrial activity that began no less than **seven thousand years ago**. The result is a unique humanized landscape, where popular architecture blends with traditional salt engineering.

Through more than four kilometers of wooden pipes —built with pine, stone, and clay— the saltwater from the springs travels naturally to the stepped terraces. There, the action of the sun and wind crystallizes the salt in the pans, keeping alive an ancestral process that is a testimony to human intelligence at the service of nature.
