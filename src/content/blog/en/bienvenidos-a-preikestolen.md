This week I leave you with an entry in which the Norwegian tourist office describes what the climb to the Pulpit Rock is like, one of the excursions we can do on the cruise scheduled for May 26 through the Norwegian fjords.

**Hiking route to the Pulpit Rock (Preikestolen).** The Pulpit Rock, or Preikestolen, rises 604 meters above the Lysefjord. The hiking route that leads there is one of the most famous in Norway. The best proof of the interest generated by this excursion is the more than 300,000 people who visit the well-known rock formation every year.

**Four-hour hike.** The route to Preikestolen is eight kilometers. The round trip takes about four hours. The trail has an elevation of about 500 meters and some sections are quite steep. The hike is of medium difficulty.
