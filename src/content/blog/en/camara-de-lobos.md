**Câmara de Lobos** is one of the most iconic and picturesque places on the island of Madeira, Portugal. It is a traditional fishing village located just a few kilometers west of Funchal, the capital. Here are the highlights for your visit:

**The Seaside Essence.** The most characteristic feature of Câmara de Lobos is its beautiful bay, always filled with traditional colorful fishing boats (known as xavelhas). It is a very lively and authentic place, where you can still see fishermen working or playing cards near the port.

**The Connection with Winston Churchill.** This village gained international fame when Sir Winston Churchill visited in 1950. He was so captivated by the beauty of the bay that he sat there to paint the landscape. Today, there is a plaque and a statue commemorating his visit, and the viewpoint from which he painted is popularly known as the "Winston Churchill Viewpoint."
