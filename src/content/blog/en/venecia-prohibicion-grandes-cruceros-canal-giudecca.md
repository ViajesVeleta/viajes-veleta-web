The images of giant ships crossing the **Venice** lagoon and passing just meters from the Doge's Palace are coming to an end. The Italian Government has approved a decree to progressively move large vessels—both passenger and freight—away from the fragile waters surrounding the city of canals.

This decision addresses the historical demands of Venetians, environmental organizations, and **UNESCO**, given the risk of erosion to submerged foundations and the landscape impact on a city declared a World Heritage Site. The Giudecca Canal has suffered for years from the transit of these steel titans, and their absence during the pandemic has shown a clear improvement in the water quality of the lagoon.
