**Palencia's Romanesque**

**Aguilar de Campoo**

Dominated from the Peña Aguilón by a 12th-century castle, the town of Aguilar de Campoo is a key point in the itineray of Palencia's Romanesque architecture. Among its most suggestive corners, the arcaded square stands out, where the collegiate church of San Miguel is located. This 14th-century temple is endowed with a museum that includes the parochial treasure with goldsmithing, paintings, and ivories. No less important is the monastery of Santa María la Real, a beautiful proto-Gothic specimen included among the oldest in Spain. Inside its church, a museum of romanesque art is located, with rich and varied content. And, finally, the hermitage of Santa Cecilia, equipped with a charismatic tower.
