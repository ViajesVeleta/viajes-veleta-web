*Hagia Sophia* is one of the most important and impressive buildings in the world. Built as a **church** in the 6th century and converted into a **mosque** by the Ottomans, it has been standing for more than 1,500 years. Today it remains a functioning mosque and a true **symbol of Istanbul**.

The **Hagia Sophia** (*Mosque of Santa Sophia*) in Istanbul, Turkey, is a masterpiece of architecture and symbolizes a unique historical milestone. Originally built as a church in 537 AD, it became a mosque after 1453 and, in 1935, was transformed into a museum. In 2020 it began functioning as a mosque again, although it remains open to visitors of all beliefs. Its mix of Byzantine and Ottoman-Muslim elements, its impressive mosaics, and its 31-meter diameter dome make it a must-see. Located in **Sultanahmet**, it is close to other historical attractions such as the **Blue Mosque** and the **Topkapi Palace**.
