One of the mandatory rituals when visiting the majestic temples of **Angkor** in Siem Reap is watching the sunset from the temple of **Phnom Bakheng**. To do this, you must climb a gentle hill that rises about 70 meters above the surrounding plain and find a spot among the crowd patiently awaiting the moment the light begins to fade.

The color spectacle that unfolds over the Cambodian jungle is, quite simply, guaranteed. But beyond the visual beauty, there is a mystical component: according to local Buddhist tradition, by performing this small sunset pilgrimage, you gain the favor of an unknown god who, from that moment on, is unconditionally on your side. We don't know if the deity kept their word, but the chromatic gift and the peace of that moment among millennia-old stones were well worth the wait.
