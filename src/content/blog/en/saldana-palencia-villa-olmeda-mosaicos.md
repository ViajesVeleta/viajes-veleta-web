In our journey through the lands of **Palencia**, a mandatory stop is the town of **Saldaña**, located in the Saldaña-Valdavia region. Among its typically Castilian houses stands the Church of San Pedro, which today serves as the headquarters of the **Monographic Museum of the Villa de La Olmeda**.

Precisely, in neighboring Pedrosa de la Vega, you can find the **Villa Romana de La Olmeda** archaeological site, a jewel from the 3rd century AD. One of the most formidable collections of Latin mosaics in the peninsula is preserved here, featuring figurative and vegetal scenes of amazing realism. Next to the main villa, you can also visit the thermal baths and its necropolis, a direct trip to the splendor of the Roman Empire in the middle of the Castilian plateau.
