The French city of **Biarritz** has been, for over a century, synonymous with elegance and fine living facing the rough waters of the Bay of Biscay. This sunset scene reflects the beauty of a destination that became the favorite refuge of European aristocracy thanks to the Spanish-born Empress Eugénie de Montijo.

In the mid-19th century, Eugénie transformed this former fishing village into the epicenter of coastal luxury, commissioning an imposing palace that attracted the continent's wealthiest classes. Today, Biarritz maintains that stately character, blending it with a vibrant cultural scene, perfect surfing beaches, and an atmosphere of relaxation that invites unhurried strolls along its boulevards and maritime viewpoints. A place where the sound of the waves and imperial architecture merge in an eternal embrace.
