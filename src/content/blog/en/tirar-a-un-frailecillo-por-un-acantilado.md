**Throwing a puffin off a cliff: the Icelandic tradition that helps these birds continue their life cycle.**

Far from being a violent act, this ancestral custom is part of a strategy to help these seabirds find their way.

On the coasts of **Iceland**, every late summer marks the beginning of a peculiar ritual: throwing young **puffins** from the top of the cliffs. The tradition, known as *puffling rescue*, may sound cruel, but it is quite the opposite. Far from being an act of violence, it constitutes an ancestral strategy to help these seabirds find their way to the sea.

Juvenile puffins, confused by the artificial lights of the villages, often become disoriented and end up in the streets, unable to resume flight. In response, locals organize to rescue them, collecting them in boxes and taking them back to the cliffs. There, they throw them toward the ocean, thus giving them back the opportunity to continue their life cycle. This community act reflects a deep bond between Icelanders and their natural environment: participating in the release of puffins not only preserves the species but also transmits values of care and environmental responsibility.
