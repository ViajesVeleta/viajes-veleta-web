Located in the heart of **Castilla-La Mancha**, **Villanueva de los Infantes** boasts one of the most outstanding architectural ensembles of the Spanish Golden Age. Its streets are full of noble houses, 16th and 17th-century palaces, and religious buildings of astonishing richness.

A key point on the **Quixote** route —some studies point to it as the true "place in La Mancha"— the town is also the place of eternal rest for **Francisco de Quevedo**, buried in the church of San Andrés. Strolling through its Plaza Mayor and discovering buildings like the Alhóndiga or the Casa de los Estudios is to immerse yourself in the living history of Spanish literature and art.
