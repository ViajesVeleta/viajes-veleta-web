Settled at the mouth of the Bidasoa River, the natural border with France, **Hondarribia** (Fuenterrabía) is a city that combines long maritime tradition with an exceptionally rich historical legacy. Its **walled historical center**, filled with noble buildings bearing coats of arms and cobbled squares, has been declared a Historic-Artistic Monument.

Tradition says it was founded in the 6th century by the Visigothic King Reccared, and the city has witnessed multiple sieges over the centuries due to its strategic military position. Today, those solid walls protect an urban ensemble where the Castle of Charles V stands out. But Hondarribia has another equally fascinating side: the **La Marina** neighborhood. With its typical fisherman's houses in vibrant colors and flower-filled balconies, it is the ideal place to enjoy Basque gastronomy in all its splendor, making this city an essential stop on the Gipuzkoan coast.
