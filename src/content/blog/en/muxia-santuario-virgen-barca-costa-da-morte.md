The cultural history of the town of **Muxía** is inextricably linked to the **Sanctuary of the Virgin of A Barca**. This place, an object of magic-religious worship throughout the centuries, is the expected reward for the thousands of pilgrims who travel the Jacobean Way from Santiago. Legend has it that the Virgin arrived here in a stone boat to encourage the Apostle James in his evangelization.

The site is spectacular, with an atrium extending towards the sea where the famous **magic stones** rest, remnants of that mythical vessel. The *Pedra de Abalar* or the *Pedra de Os Cadrís* are visited for their supposed healing properties. During our trip through the Rías Altas, we were able to witness the spiritual strength of this location where faith blends with the wildness of the Atlantic Ocean.
