The architectural roots of **Cordoba** inevitably lead back to the Caliphate era. Its great emblem, the Mosque, began to be built in the 8th century and was for centuries the second largest in the world.

Today, its interior still impresses with the hypnotic polychrome horseshoe arches and the beauty of its **Qibla**, which captivates with its filigree and details. It is a unique building where history overlaps in every corner, from its Islamic origin to its subsequent adaptation as a Christian cathedral.
