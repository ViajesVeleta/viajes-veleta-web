Standing before the **Angkor Wat** temple at dawn is one of those experiences that define a trip through Southeast Asia. This activity, which for many takes on an almost spiritual quality, gathers numerous people every day who are willing to wake up at four in the morning to witness an unparalleled spectacle of light and shadow.

The moment the sun begins to peek from behind the temple's five central towers—representing Mount Meru from Hindu mythology—is shockingly beautiful. As the sky clears, the silhouette of this Khmer architectural jewel is reflected in the calm waters of its frontal ponds, creating perfect symmetry. Despite the hundreds of fellow travelers surrounding you, the moment of the first ray of sunlight is usually met with reverent silence, reminding us why Angkor Wat remains the heart and pride of Cambodia.
