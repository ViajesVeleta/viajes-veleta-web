Surrounded by Sierra Nevada and dominated by the twisting earth of the *badlands*, the **Hoya de Guadix** is one of the most unknown and fascinating corners of Andalusia. It was called "the red land" by 19th-century romantic travelers, who found in this broken landscape an exoticism reminiscent of the American Wild West.

This geological enclave, declared a World Geopark by UNESCO, offers an explosion of ocher and reddish colors that change with the daylight. Places like **Los Coloraos** in Gorafe or the **Mirador del Fin del Mundo** in Beas de Guadix allow you to contemplate the extreme erosion that has shaped sharp ridges, canyons, and vertical ravines, offering a vista that could well pass for the Arizona desert.
