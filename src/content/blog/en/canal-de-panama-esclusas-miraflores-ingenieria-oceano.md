The **Panama Canal** is undoubtedly one of the most consequential works of modern history. Although the idea of a waterway connecting the Atlantic and Pacific already occupied the minds of Spanish explorers, it wasn't until the late 19th century that the dream began to materialize, after overcoming immense technical and human challenges.

Traversing this 68-kilometer waterway is a fascinating experience. In this photograph, we see a merchant ship passing through the **Miraflores locks**, beginning its transit from the Pacific Ocean toward the Atlantic. The scale is overwhelming: between 35 and 40 ships cross every day on a journey lasting about 24 hours, with tolls that can reach astronomical figures—such as the $317,000 paid in 2008. Yet beyond the numbers, what remains impressive is seeing how human ingenuity has been able to bend geography to unite two worlds, making Panama the great logistical navel of the planet.
