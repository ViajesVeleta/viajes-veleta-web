Located in the heart of La Mancha, **Consuegra** is one of the most charming towns in the region. It was a highlight of our last group trip, offering that iconic image everyone associates with the adventures of the Ingenious Gentleman Don Quixote of La Mancha.

At the foot of **Cerro Calderico** stand twelve majestic windmills and an impressive medieval castle. Five of these windmills retain their complete original mechanisms, most notably "Sancho," named after the faithful squire. The castle, granted to the Order of Saint John of Jerusalem during the reign of Alfonso VIII, boasts a double line of walls and an exceptional keep. But Consuegra is much more than its "giants"; its Plaza de España, with the Renaissance City Hall and houses with traditional wooden balconies, invites you to wander through this town of Roman roots that breathes history at every turn.
