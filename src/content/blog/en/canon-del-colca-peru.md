**Fascinating birds, breathtaking landscapes, the spectacular Colca Valley, and the lightness of altitude in the Peruvian Andes.**

Imagine a valley of interplanetary dimensions, a Vernian stage with centuries of hidden history. Setting out from Arequipa, we climb from 2,300 to 5,000 meters: coca tea for breakfast is a must to handle the altitude.

The road snakes through shifting landscapes, from arid deserts and cacti to the Salinas and Aguada Blanca National Reserve, home to vicuñas and guanacos. After reaching the Andes viewpoint at 4,910 meters, we descend to the town of Chivay, in the heart of the Colca Valley.

The major highlight, however, is the Condor. At the Cruz del Cóndor viewpoint, you can witness the majestic flight of these legendary birds, which soar through a 1,500-meter-deep canyon with their three-meter wingspan.
