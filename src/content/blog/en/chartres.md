Today I leave you a brief description of one of the visits made on our trip through French Brittany: **Chartres**.

Chartres, the capital of Beauce, is famous for the Cathedral of Notre-Dame, a religious building declared a UNESCO World Heritage site. Situated on a promontory overlooking the river Eure, Chartres Cathedral constitutes an authentic masterpiece of Gothic art. This prominent place of pilgrimage is one of the largest Gothic cathedrals in France. It has been wonderfully preserved and, due to the speed of its construction, presents a great architectural homogeneity. The facades adorned with thousands of sculptures, and especially the Royal portal, arouse admiration. The fascination continues inside the building. The cathedral has extraordinary stained-glass windows from the 12th and 13th centuries, as well as a magnificent choir enclosure.

After visiting the cathedral and the crypt, visitors will be able to see a large number of old houses in the old town, such as the Salmon House (16th century), the Vault House or the houses on the Rue des Écuyers. It is also recommended to stroll through the old tanners' quarter, along the river Eure, dotted with bridges and overpasses. From there, you can behold a beautiful view of the church of Saint-André, a Romanesque building that now houses an exhibition center. After climbing the nearby and picturesque Saint-Nicolas hill, visitors will discover the beautiful terraced gardens of the former bishopric overlooking the banks of the Eure.
