Wandering through **Old Havana** means coming across characters who seem to have stepped out of another era. This portrait captures an elderly woman enjoying a cigar, an image that symbolizes the deep-rooted tobacco culture on the island.

If you travel to Cuba and share this interest, or simply want to bring back a souvenir, a fundamental tip is to be cautious with street purchases. Almost everything offered on the street will be low-quality tobacco disguised as authentic Habanos. To ensure you purchase genuine, quality pieces, it's always best to visit official state stores or buy them at airport duty-free shops before departing. This way, you guarantee experiencing the true aroma and flavor that has made Habanos famous worldwide.
