**UNESCO in Sri Lanka: Cultural Triangle**

Sri Lanka has eight sites on the UNESCO World Heritage list. In the north, Polonnaruwa stands out with its artificial gardens, the legendary Sigiriya rock, and the dagobas of Anuradhapura.

In the south, **Galle** is especially noteworthy. Built by Portuguese colonizers in the 16th century, it is the best-preserved example of a European fortress in all of South Asia. Walking along its ramparts at sunset is one of the most beautiful experiences on the island.

Near the southern area is also Kandy, the city that holds the Buddha's tooth, and the Sinharaja Forest Reserve, which preserves the island's virgin tropical rainforest.
