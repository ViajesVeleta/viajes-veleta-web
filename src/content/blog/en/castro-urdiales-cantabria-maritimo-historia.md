Anciently known as **Flavióbriga** due to its origins as a Roman settlement, **Castro Urdiales** possesses the unmistakable charm of a fishing village with a long seafaring tradition. Its old quarter, declared a Historic Site in 1978, preserves its medieval essence with narrow streets and characteristic houses featuring wooden balconies that invite leisurely strolls.

The town's most iconic view is formed by the church of **Santa María**—the finest example of Cantabrian Gothic (13th to 15th centuries)—and the emblematic **lighthouse-castle**, visible from every corner. Yet the history of Castro Urdiales stretches much further back: from Paleolithic engravings in the Peña del Cuco cave to the Roman site of Flavióbriga sleeping beneath the current city's subsoil. Its beautiful beaches, such as Brazomar or Urdiales, complete the allure of this indispensable Cantabrian destination.
