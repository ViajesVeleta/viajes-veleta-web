In a remote corner of the **Petén** jungle, in northern **Guatemala**, rise the imposing Mayan temples of **Tikal**. This extraordinarily restored archaeological complex amazes with its size and architectural skill, featuring pyramids that seem like almost impossible angles emerging from the thick vegetation.

Tikal was one of the most powerful kingdoms of the Mayan civilization for 1,600 years. Walking through its squares, such as the **Great Plaza** or the North Acropolis, allows you to imagine the splendor of a city that once housed thousands of people. If you walk in silence, only the cry of spider monkeys and the passage of coatis under the trees break the peace of these relics. Don't miss climbing **Temple IV**, the second tallest pre-Columbian building in the hemisphere, to enjoy a fabulous view over the endless green tapestry of the jungle.
