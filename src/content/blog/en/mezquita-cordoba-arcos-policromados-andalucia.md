The architectural roots of **Cordoba** inevitably trace back to its Caliphate past. In the heart of the city stands its grand emblem: the **Mosque-Cathedral**, which for centuries was the second-largest mosque in the world after Mecca.

Begun in the 8th century, this temple continues to impress with its hypnotic forest of columns and iconic polychrome horseshoe arches. Despite subsequent transformations, the original structure retains a unique magic, especially in its **Qibla**, laden with intricate filigree and details that have fascinated travelers for generations. It is, undoubtedly, one of the most emblematic buildings not only in Andalusia but in all of Spain.
