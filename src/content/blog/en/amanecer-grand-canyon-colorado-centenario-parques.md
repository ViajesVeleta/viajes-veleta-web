This month marks the centenary of the creation of the National Park Service in the United States, a political decision with a clear goal: to preserve landscapes of incalculable beauty for future generations. The **Grand Canyon of the Colorado River** is undoubtedly the crown jewel of this system, which today manages over 338,000 square kilometers of protected wilderness.

This week's photograph was taken early in the morning from one of the park's overlooks. Watching the first rays of sunlight begin to illuminate the deep crevices and sedimentary layers of the canyon is a breathtaking experience. It is a reminder of the vastness of geological time and the wisdom it took, one hundred years ago, to decide that such places should remain untouched for the enjoyment of all who would follow.
