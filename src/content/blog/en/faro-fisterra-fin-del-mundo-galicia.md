Located at the top of Cape **Fisterra**, almost 140 meters above the Atlantic, this lighthouse is one of the most legendary in Europe. A tireless guardian of the **Costa da Morte** (Death Coast), its image atop the cliffs is a universal symbol for sailors and pilgrims alike.

Built in 1853, the Fisterra lighthouse not only guides ships with its 65-kilometer range but also marks the spiritual end of the **Camino de Santiago**. Today, in addition to fulfilling its function, the complex houses an information center and the O Semáforo hotel, a unique spot with infinite views to disconnect and listen to the pulse of the ocean.
