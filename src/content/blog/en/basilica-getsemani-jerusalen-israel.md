The Basilica of **Gethsemane**, also known as the Church of All Nations or the Basilica of the Agony, is a Catholic church located on the Mount of Olives in Jerusalem, next to the Garden of Gethsemane. Inside lies the rock where, according to tradition, Jesus prayed on the night of his arrest.

The current building, constructed between 1919 and 1924, rests on the foundations of a 4th-century Byzantine basilica and a 12th-century Crusader chapel. Its name "Church of All Nations" refers to the funding contributed by many different countries, whose symbols appear in the ceiling mosaics. The Neo-Byzantine facade is crowned with a large mosaic symbolically showing Jesus as the link between God and humanity.
