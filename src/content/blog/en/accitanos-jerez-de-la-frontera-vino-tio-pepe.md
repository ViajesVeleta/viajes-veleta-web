Every year, taking advantage of the Andalusia Day long weekend, we set out on a short trip to discover corners of our country. In 2015, our focus was on the Doñana National Park, Sanlúcar de Barrameda, and the noble city of **Jerez de la Frontera**.

Jerez is synonymous with world-renowned wine, and there's no better way to understand its history than by visiting one of its wineries. We enjoyed the hospitality at González Byass, where we learned the secrets of their winemaking process. In the heart of the city, we posed with one of the great local symbols: the statue of **Tío Pepe**, the world's most famous Fino Sherry brand. The friendly nature of its people and the quality of its wines make Jerez an essential stop for any lover of Andalusian culture and gastronomy.
