**Prague**, one of Europe's most beautiful cities, is famous for its medieval architecture and historic bridges. However, the curious traveler can find contemporary art jewels that challenge the traditional gaze, such as the unique bust of **Franz Kafka**.

This impressive steel sculpture, the work of provocative Czech artist **David Černý**, rises eleven meters above the ground. Most fascinating is its structure: 42 movable levels that rotate independently, deconstructing and recomposing the writer's face in a hypnotic cadence. Weighing 39 tons, this piece of engineering and art reflects the complexity of the Kafkaesque universe in the heart of his hometown. In the image, we capture a moment with the Viajes Jairán group during our visit in the spring of 2017.
