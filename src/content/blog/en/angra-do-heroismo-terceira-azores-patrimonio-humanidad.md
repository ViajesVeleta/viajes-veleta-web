Located on the island of Terceira—the third of the Azores to be discovered by 15th-century navigators—**Angra do Heroismo** was the first city of this archipelago strategically anchored in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean.

It gained vital importance as a commercial hub and mandatory stopover on the shipping routes between Europe and the Americas. Its rich history and the heritage built over the centuries have meant that its town center is now a **World Heritage Site**. What makes Angra truly special, much like other Azorean towns, is the fascinating contrast between the lush nature and the dark volcanic stone used in its elegant buildings. Strolling through its streets reveals a unique chromatic harmony where urban design and geology shake hands under the Atlantic sea breeze.
