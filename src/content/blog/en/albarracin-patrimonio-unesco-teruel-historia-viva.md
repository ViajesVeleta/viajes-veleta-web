It never fails. Every time a list of the most beautiful villages in Spain is published, **Albarracín** invariably appears in the top positions. Named a National Monument in 1961 and recipient of the Gold Medal for Merit in Fine Arts, this Turolian village is currently proposed for UNESCO World Heritage status due to its exceptional layout and preservation.

A stroll through its cobbled streets, where time seems to have stood still for centuries, is an unavoidable duty on any visit to the province of Teruel. If possible, it's worth exploring with a good guide who can reveal the secrets of its defensive architecture and the stories of the families who lived in these pinkish-plastered houses. Albarracín is living proof that not only does Teruel exist, but it also guards some of the most valuable treasures of the Iberian Peninsula.
