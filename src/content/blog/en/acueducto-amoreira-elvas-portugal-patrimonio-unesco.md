During our trip through Portugal, we made an essential stop at the fortified city of **Elvas**. After a stroll through its historical center, we were awe-struck by its most imposing monument: the **Amoreira Aqueduct**. This amazing engineering feat is part of the "Garrison Border Town of Elvas and its Fortifications," which were declared a **UNESCO** World Heritage site in 2012.

With its 8.5 kilometers in length and 843 superimposed arches, it is considered the largest aqueduct on the Iberian Peninsula. Its pillars rise up to 31 meters high, supporting a structure that combines underground galleries with monumental exterior arches reinforced by buttresses. Connecting the town of Amoreira to Elvas, this construction stands as a testament to the strategic importance and architectural ambition of this Alentejo border region.
