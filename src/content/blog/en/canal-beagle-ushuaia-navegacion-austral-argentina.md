The **Beagle Channel** is much more than a simple maritime strait; it is the threshold of Antarctica and one of the most fascinating journeys one can undertake from **Ushuaia**. This natural passage, linking the Atlantic and Pacific oceans south of Tierra del Fuego, offers one of the Southern Hemisphere's most beautiful and savage scenes.

It received its name in honor of the British brig "HMS Beagle," which explored these coasts in the 19th century under Commander Robert FitzRoy, with a young **Charles Darwin** aboard. Navigating its waters, which reach depths of 200 meters off Ushuaia Bay, is to discover a labyrinth of islands and islets populated by sea lions and southern birds. Its "U"-shaped cross-section is the legacy of ancient glaciations that sculpted this landscape of moraines and ice-polished rocky islands. It is, undoubtedly, an essential experience for any traveler reaching the southernmost city in the world.
