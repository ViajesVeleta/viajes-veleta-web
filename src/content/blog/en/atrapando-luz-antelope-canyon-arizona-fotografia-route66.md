The **Antelope Canyon**, in Arizona, offers another type of confinement: that of light trapped between sandstone walls. This slot canyon, sculpted over millennia by the erosive action of water, presents itself as a long corridor of sinuous forms that evoke a petrified sea of reddish tones.

When the sun reaches its highest point over the desert, light beams filter through the narrow upper openings, creating beautiful curtains and shafts of light that seem to dance in the dust-laden air. This photograph, taken during our unforgettable trip along **Route 66**, is undoubtedly an essential pilgrimage site for any photography enthusiast seeking to capture the essence of American geology in its purest and most artistic state.
