In the **Royal Palace of Ayutthaya** complex, the former capital of Siam, visitors encounter a fascinating architectural surprise. Unlike other monumental sites in the country, here typical Thai architecture blends harmoniously with purely Western influences.

As shown in this week's photo, elegant local structures coexist with buildings constructed in the likeness of those at the Palace of Versailles in France. This unique combination reflects the Thai monarchy's desire to open up to the world and modernize the country without losing its roots. Strolling through its gardens and contemplating this fusion of styles is a lesson in history and diplomacy etched in stone, wood, and gold, in a setting that still breathes the grandeur of a vanished kingdom.
