Close to Flagstaff, a must-stop on the way to the Grand Canyon, the small town of **Sedona** offers one of the most baffling and beautiful landscapes in the United States. While its center is filled with tourist services, its true charm lies in the immensity of the rock formations that surround it.

The shades ranging from brown to deep red create a relief capable of stunning anyone. To fully enjoy it, I recommend its multiple hiking trails or taking a tour of the famous **vortices** (such as Airport Mesa), points considered by many as authentic sources of positive energy. In winter, the mountain system is tinged with white, while in spring and autumn, the setting offers an endless range of colors. A magical stop on any trip along **Route 66**.
