Beyond the imposing stone faces guarding the royal city of **Angkor Thom**, its canals preserve scenes of daily life that seem unchanged by the centuries. In this week's image, we can see duckweed harvesters working along its banks.

Surprisingly, this is one of the few canals in the area where the water is not clean and clear, covered instead by a green mantle of aquatic vegetation. However, this does not detract from the spectacular nature of the surroundings. These canals, which once formed part of the Khmer Empire's complex hydraulic system, remain a source of life and work for the region's inhabitants today, offering the traveler an authentic and necessary glimpse that complements the monumental grandeur of the neighboring temples.
