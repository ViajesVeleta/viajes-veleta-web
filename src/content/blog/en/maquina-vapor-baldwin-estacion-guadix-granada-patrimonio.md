The railway station in **Guadix**, in the province of Granada, houses one of the most precious gems for railway enthusiasts: the **Baldwin** steam locomotive. This imposing machine, manufactured in the United States, is a living reminder of the golden age of trains in Spain.

Installed at the station as a museum piece, the Baldwin transports us to an era where coal and steam were the engines of progress. Its imposing black iron presence contrasts with the clay lands of the Accitana region, offering a scene laden with nostalgia and power. It is a necessary tribute to the area's industrial history and an essential stop for those visiting this city of caves and cathedrals.
