**Santa María Square** is undoubtedly the beating heart of the medieval city of Cáceres. This space, surrounded by palaces and churches that seem to have frozen time, is the ultimate expression of the splendor enjoyed by the Extremaduran nobility for centuries.

Walking across its stones is to transport oneself to an era of knights and legends. Presided over by the Co-Cathedral that gives it its name, the square has witnessed the most important moments of public life in Cáceres. The sobriety of its stone facades and the atmosphere of peace breathed in its corners make this place a mandatory stop for anyone wishing to understand why the historic center of Cáceres is recognized as one of the best-preserved in all of Europe. It is, quite simply, the essence of civil and religious architecture merged in an embrace of granite and sky.
