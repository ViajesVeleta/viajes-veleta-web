**Aragon**

**Teruel**

The Mudéjar architecture of Aragon was included in the UNESCO list with the towers and the cathedral of Teruel in 1986, and 15 years later it was expanded to eight buildings in four more municipalities of the autonomous community: Zaragoza, Calatayud, Tobed, and Cervera de la Cañada.

The Mudéjar art, present between the 12th and 17th centuries, is unique in the world and is characterized above all by the influence of Islamic art, perfectly perceptible in its ornamentation. For this, a refined use of brick and glazed ceramics is made, especially in the bell towers.
