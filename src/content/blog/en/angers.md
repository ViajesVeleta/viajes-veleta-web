Angers is a historic city in the Loire Valley (France), part of the cultural landscape declared a UNESCO World Heritage site, which stands out for its mix of medieval heritage, cultural life, and green spaces. Its most emblematic symbol is the **Château d’Angers**, a 13th-century fortress with 17 towers that houses the *Apocalypse Tapestry*, the most extensive and outstanding collection of medieval tapestries in the world (103 m long). The historic center, next to the Maine River, invites you to stroll through streets with Gothic architecture like the *Saint-Maurice Cathedral* and traditional half-timbered houses.

The cultural offer includes several quality museums: the *Musée des Beaux-Arts* with European painting and sculpture, the *Muséum* of Natural Sciences with large collections of natural history, and botanical spaces like the *Jardin des Plantes* or the *Gaston Allard Arboretum*. Nearby is **Terra Botanica**, a botanical theme park that combines shows, gardens, and educational experiences about nature.
