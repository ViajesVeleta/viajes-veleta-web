**Angers** is a historic city in the **Loire Valley** (France), part of the cultural landscape designated as a **UNESCO World Heritage Site**, renowned for its blend of medieval heritage, cultural life, and green spaces.

Its most iconic landmark is the **Château d'Angers**, a 13th-century fortress with 17 towers that houses the **Apocalypse Tapestry**, the largest and most significant collection of medieval tapestries in the world (*103 m long*). The historic city centre, along the **Maine** river, invites visitors to stroll through streets lined with Gothic architecture such as the **Saint-Maurice Cathedral** and traditional half-timbered houses.

The cultural offering includes several outstanding museums: the **Musée des Beaux-Arts** with European paintings and sculpture, the **Muséum** of natural history with extensive natural history collections, and botanical spaces such as the **Jardin des Plantes** and the **Arboretum Gaston Allard**. Nearby is **Terra Botanica**, a botanical theme park combining shows, gardens, and educational experiences about the natural world.
