The **Royal Palace of Rabat** is a mandatory stop on any Moroccan tour. Although security reasons prevent entry to its interior and there are strict restrictions on photography, its exterior magnificence is bound to impress any visitor.

Contrary to common belief, this palace is not the monarch's usual residence but the administrative epicenter housing the seat of government and various public institutions. The complex is a true city within the city, even housing a mosque and its madrasa. To reach it, one crosses the **Méchouar** esplanade, a monumental square with seven royal gates, each uniquely decorated. In this image, captured with a telephoto lens, we witness the solemn changing of the guard, a ceremony featuring representatives from the three armed forces: Army, Navy, and Air Force, reflecting the tradition and protocol of the Alouite kingdom.
