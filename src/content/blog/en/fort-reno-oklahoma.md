**Fort Reno, Oklahoma.**

**Historic Fort Reno** is a key point of interest in Oklahoma, located near El Reno on the historic **Route 66**. Initially established as a military camp in 1874 to protect the Cheyenne-Arapahoe Indian Agency, it was formally created as a fort a year later. It operated until 1948, serving during that time as a U.S. Cavalry post during the Indian Wars.

Later, it became an important Army Remount Station for training horses and mules. A significant chapter of its history is its use as a **prisoner of war camp** for German and Italian soldiers during World War II. Today, the site features a **Visitor Center and Museum** displaying artifacts from all these eras, including information on the "Buffalo Soldiers" and frontier life. In addition to its historical tours, the fort is famous for its Spirit Tours, given its reputation for being haunted.
