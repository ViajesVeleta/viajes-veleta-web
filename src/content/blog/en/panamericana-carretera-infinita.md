The **Pan-American Highway** is the crystallization of a dream: to unite an entire continent by land. With more than 30,000 kilometers, it connects 14 countries from Prudhoe Bay in Alaska to Buenos Aires (or Ushuaia, for the purists).

Throughout its route, it crosses two hemispheres, various time zones, and ecosystems ranging from Arctic tundra to arid deserts and tropical jungles. The only point where the road is interrupted is the **Darien Gap**, an impenetrable jungle between Panama and Colombia that remains the ultimate challenge for any adventurer.
