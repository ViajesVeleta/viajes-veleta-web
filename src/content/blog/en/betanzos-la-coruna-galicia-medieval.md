Just before the 2020 lockdown, we had the fortune of visiting **Betanzos** during a tour of the Galician Rías Altas. This city, which was once the capital of one of the seven provinces of the ancient Kingdom of Galicia, left a wonderful impression on us with its authenticity and beauty.

Its historic center, declared a historic-artistic site in 1970, is one of the best examples of medieval architecture in Galicia. Among its gems is the church of **Santa María del Azougue**, a Gothic temple declared a national monument and an essential stop. Bathed by the Mandeo, Mendo, and Mero rivers, Betanzos offers an urban and natural landscape well worth discovering, not to mention its well-deserved gastronomic fame (especially its famous Spanish omelet).
