Rome always surprises, but few things are as striking as the **Pantheon**. This 2nd-century building, located in the lively Piazza della Rotonda, is an architectural engineering gem that has remained almost intact for two millennia. Its name, meaning \"temple of all gods,\" reflects Emperor Hadrian's ambition to create a universal sanctuary.

What truly leaves visitors breathless is its spectacular **dome**. With a central oculus 9 meters in diameter that allows light to pass through, the structure is a marvel of balance and lightweight materials, such as volcanic stone. Without the need for visible support elements, the Pantheon has fascinated architects of all eras, keeping under its dome the secret of a technique that seems to defy time.
