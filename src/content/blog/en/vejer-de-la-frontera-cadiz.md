Low white houses, cobbled and steep streets, hidden Nasrid courtyards, and panoramas that reach the Strait. **Vejer de la Frontera** seduces at first sight with its blinding light and labyrinthine layout.

Although Romans, Phoenicians, and Carthaginians passed through its walls, it is the Moorish imprint that dominated this strategic enclave for over five centuries. Walking through Vejer in autumn, when the heat subsides and its narrow streets become silent, is to discover a perfect blend of architecture, history, and a gastronomy marked by bluefin tuna.
