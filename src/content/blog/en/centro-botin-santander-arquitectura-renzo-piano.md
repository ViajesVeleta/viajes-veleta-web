The **Centro Botín** has established itself as an essential visit in the city of Santander. This masterpiece by architect **Renzo Piano** (Pritzker Prize winner in 1998) stands out for its ability to integrate art with the maritime environment of the bay.

The building consists of two volumes of different sizes that appear to float on columns, partially suspended over the sea. Its distinctive ceramic facade is covered with 280,000 disc-shaped pieces that reflect light and water ripples, giving it a unique visual lightness. While the west volume is entirely dedicated to art with large exhibition halls, the east volume focuses on educational activities and features a spectacular panoramic terrace. Seamlessly integrated with the **Pereda Gardens**, the Centro Botín is a brilliant example of how modern architecture can revitalize and enhance the waterfront of a historic city.
