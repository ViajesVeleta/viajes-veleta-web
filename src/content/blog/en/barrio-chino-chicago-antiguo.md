A must-visit in **Chicago** is its Chinatown. Its development began in the mid-19th century with the arrival of immigrants from China and various regions of the United States. Although it was initially located in the Loop, it soon moved to its current location in **Armour Square** due to rising rents and social tensions of the time.

Walking through **Chicago's Chinatown**, you immerse yourself in a vibrant environment full of shops with products brought directly from China. The neighborhood offers everything from traditional pharmacies practicing acupuncture to an incredible variety of authentic restaurants. Being one of the oldest Chinatowns in the U.S., it is a cultural experience not to be missed.
