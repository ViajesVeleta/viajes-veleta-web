**Asilah, the golden Moroccan city that smiles at the waves and distances itself from poses**

The coastal municipality is full of authentic charms and structures, such as its imposing wall, its great mosque, and its beautiful viewpoints, which have attracted great names in literature and music.

Far from being a protective sky, in the style of [Paul Bowles](https://elpais.com/elpais/2017/04/23/viajero_astuto/1492980996_320035.html), the old Portuguese wall of [Asilah](https://elpais.com/elviajero/2016/09/29/actualidad/1475158379_512788.html) does not evoke omens of ruptures. Golden and somewhat presumptuous at dusk, that stone shell smiles at the waves that reach almost the feet of the current medina. Few ancient Moroccan cities can be proud of beating at the same level as the waves. And on top of that, the medina of Asilah is accessed by three very solemn doors. The main one is Bab al-Bahr, the Sea Gate, Porta de Ribeira in Lusitanian times. There the great white and blue labyrinth begins immediately, like a succession of paintings by Matisse.

The good thing about the medina is that people don't pose. There is a tailor working at street level. Another sells babouches in the small interior souk. The old city beats with its schools and its mosques. The exoticism is greatly moderated when normality is given by wearing a djellaba.
