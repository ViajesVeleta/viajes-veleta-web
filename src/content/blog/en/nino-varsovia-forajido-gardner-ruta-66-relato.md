The California Gold Rush of 1848 brought a massive tide of fortune seekers, outlaws, and adventurers to the Pacific coast. Some, unable to find luck in the mines, chose a life of crime, filling the jails of remote towns. During our journey along historic **Route 66**, we stopped in the small town of **Gardner**, Illinois, where the old local jail inspired a singular story.

Local legends (purely fictional, of course) tell of the "Warsaw Kid," an outlaw specialized in robbing casinos who outplayed the best gamblers in the state. They say he managed to escape thanks to his gang and a sheriff with a significant weakness for good bourbon. Years later, the Warsaw Kid would eventually renounce his dissolute life, requesting in his will: *"I hope the Almighty Lord, when He judges me, has a bad memory."* These anecdotes, though made up, perfectly capture the romantic and rebellious spirit that still fills the air along America's most famous highway.
