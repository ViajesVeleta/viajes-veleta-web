**Iguazú National Park: The Splendor of the Atlantic Forest**

**Iguazú National Park**, which spans territories in **Argentina** and **Brazil** (*Parque Nacional do Iguaçu*), is a **natural sanctuary** and a World Heritage site recognized globally. Its main highlight is the spectacular **Iguazú Falls**, a series of more than 275 waterfalls that form the most voluminous waterfall system in the world.

The landscape is dominated by the **immeasurable power** of the **Iguazú River**, which plunges from heights of up to 80 meters, creating a constant mist that sustains the lush surrounding vegetation. The most iconic point is the **Devil's Throat** (*Garganta del Diablo*), a U-shaped cliff where the majority of the water flow is concentrated, and the roar of the falls is deafening.

Beyond the falls, the park protects a vital stretch of the **Atlantic Forest**, one of the planet's most threatened and diverse biomes. The jungle is dense and humid, featuring giant trees, lianas, and a rich variety of ferns and orchids. This vegetation serves as home to fascinating wildlife: visitors can spot **toucans**, parrots, and other vibrantly colored birds, as well as mammals like **coatis**, capuchin monkeys, and, with some luck, pumas or **jaguars**.

The park offers an extensive network of walkways and trails, such as the **Upper Circuit**, the **Lower Circuit**, and the **Macuco Trail**, allowing visitors a total **immersion experience** in nature, observing the falls from various angles and feeling the energy of the ecosystem.
