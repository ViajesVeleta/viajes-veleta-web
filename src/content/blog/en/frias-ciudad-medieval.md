**Frías: The Medieval City That Defies Gravity**

In the north of the province of **Burgos**, hidden in the **Las Merindades** region, stands **Frías**, a place that seems frozen in time. Despite its small size and sparse population, this enclave proudly holds the title of **"city"**, an honor granted in the 15th century, making it the smallest city in **Spain**.

The first thing that strikes you upon arrival is its unmistakable silhouette. The city is crowned by the imposing **Velasco Castle**, a fortress that perched precariously atop a rocky outcrop, dominating the entire valley with an authority that has lasted for centuries.

Under the shadow of the castle, the urban planning of **Frías** is surprisingly bold. Its cobblestone streets wind through the famous **Hanging Houses**. Like their "sisters" in Cuenca, these homes overlook the abyss, built right at the edge of the rock, creating an image that defies the laws of gravity. At the opposite end of the ridge, balancing the horizon, sits the **Church of San Vicente**, whose Romanesque portico silently watches over the landscape.

But the beauty of **Frías** is not limited to the heights. At the foot of the rocky mount, crossing the waters of the **Ebro** River, lies its magnificent **Medieval Bridge**. This monumental entrance stands out as one of the few in **Spain** that still preserves its central **defensive tower**, recalling the strategic and commercial importance this crossing once held.

Visiting **Frías** is like walking through a medieval film set. However, to fully enjoy the experience, it is essential to wear **comfortable shoes**, as its steep slopes require a bit of effort which is amply rewarded by the beauty of every corner.
