A crucial aspect to consider when planning a trip is the climate of the destination. Failing to do so can ruin the highest expectations, whether due to extreme heat in Egypt during August or torrential rains in the Caribbean in autumn.

In **Andalusia**, we are more than accustomed to the rigors of summer. To fight them, we change our schedules and follow the wise local advice of "walking in the shade" (echar por la sombra). This image, taken in the central **Reyes Católicos Street in Granada**, shows one of our land's most traditional and effective solutions: the installation of large awnings covering the main streets. These canvases prevent the sun from hitting pedestrians directly, keeping our "brains from frying" and allowing the city to keep vibrating even under the most scorching summer temperatures.
