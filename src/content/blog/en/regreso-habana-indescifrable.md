**Havana** has always been a cryptic place for any outsider. Cuba confronts the traveler with its elusive and hard-to-define reality, always leaving them with new questions after each visit.

Despite recent political and economic changes, the Cuban capital keeps its aura intact. Walking along the **Malecón**, getting lost in the streets of **Old Havana**, or discovering the artistic resurgence of the **San Isidro** neighborhood are enchanting experiences. Old Havana, with iconic places like the Bodeguita del Medio or the José Martí house-museum, remains the beating heart of a city that refuses to be fully deciphered.
