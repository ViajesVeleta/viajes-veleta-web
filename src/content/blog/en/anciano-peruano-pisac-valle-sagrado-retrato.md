In the heart of the **Sacred Valley of the Incas**, the town of **Pisac** is famous not only for its impressive archaeological ruins and vibrant artisan market but also for the warmth and history emanating from its people.

This portrait captures the gaze of a Peruvian elder we encountered during our walk through the village's cobblestone streets. His face, weathered by sun and time, looks like a map of the Andean culture that has endured for centuries while keeping its traditions intact. In places like Pisac, the true essence of travel is found not just in ancient stones, but in these fleeting encounters that allow us to connect with the humanity and wisdom of a people who still proudly guard the legacy of their ancestors.
