Following an enriching getaway through the lands of Extremadura during the Andalusia Day long weekend, we return to our series of special places with the **Arco de la Estrella** (Star Arch) in **Cáceres**. This city, a World Heritage Site, welcomed us with its customary medieval elegance and exquisitely cordial hospitality.

The Arco de la Estrella, located next to the lively Plaza Mayor, is the main access point to the heart of the historical center. The photograph illustrating this entry was taken with a five-second exposure using a tripod, which gives the image an ethereal atmosphere. This shutter speed allowed the lantern light and the profile of centuries-old stones to be captured, but it also created curious "ghosts" at the bottom: these are the passersby who were walking under the arch at that precise moment. Walking through here at nightfall is to surrender to the fascination of an Extremadura that always invites you to return.
