Today I leave you a brief description (Lonely Planet) and a photograph taken during our recent trip through the Portuguese Algarve, **Cape St. Vincent.**

High cliffs, hidden sea caves, golden beaches, fringed bays and sandy stretches attract thousands of visitors to this region. Bordered by the Atlantic, the Algarve coast is a paradise for water sports, and in its abrupt interior await towns with castles, whitewashed villages, a gentle countryside upholstered with almond trees and citrus orchards.

The southwesternmost point of Europe is a spectacular place, admired even in the times of the Phoenicians. The Romans called it Promontorium Sacrum, a stretch of bare land whose current name comes from a Spanish priest martyred by the Romans. The lighthouse has a museum, but its main attraction is the cliffs, the seascapes, and the striking sunsets.
