**24 hours in Córdoba**

Walking through Córdoba is like traveling back in time. One can walk through the historic center, a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1984, and delight in the Andalusian architecture of white houses, recognizing the smell of flowers that flood patios and balconies, and occasionally hearing a classical guitar or a clapping of *simples* and *redoblás*. The meandering of its streets and some adarve (cul-de-sac) remind the traveler that, in this urban, small and manageable area, many cultural legacies exist. This is also attested by other vestiges of the city, such as the mosque-cathedral or the brotherhood between the Roman Bridge and the Calahorra Tower.

More than 2,000 years of history deserve a multi-day visit. But for those who cannot wait and want to discover Córdoba even if they do not have that time, we spell out the keys to touring it.
