An essential visit in Chicago is its **Chinatown**. This enclave began developing in the mid-19th century with the arrival of immigrants from China and various parts of the United States. Initially, the neighborhood was located in a few streets of the Loop, but due to rising rents and racial issues faced by the Chinese community, it moved to its current location in the Armour Square area.

Today, Chicago's Chinatown is one of the oldest and most authentic in the United States. Its streets are bursting with shops selling products brought directly from China, traditional pharmacies practicing acupuncture, and a gastronomic variety capable of seducing any palate. Losing oneself in its main square, under the shadow of its ornate gates and surrounded by the vitality of its inhabitants, is an experience that offers a different and fascinating perspective of the "Windy City."
