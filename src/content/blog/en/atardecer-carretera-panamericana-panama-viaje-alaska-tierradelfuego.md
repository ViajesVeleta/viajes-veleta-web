Describing the **Pan-American Highway** is an almost impossible task. While most place its start in Alaska, its end is a matter of debate: does it terminate at the enigmatic Lapataia Bay in Tierra del Fuego, or stop in Buenos Aires? Even skeptics remind us it's not a continuous route, as the indomitable Darién Gap in Panama cuts the asphalt for 100 kilometers of virgin jungle.

But mess aside, we all agree that traveling its 32,700 kilometers would be the ultimate adventure. A trip crossing borders, climates, and landscapes that would be forever etched in one's memory. It would be, in short, traversing the soul of an entire continent.

This week's photograph emerged from an unexpected moment during our journey between Bocas del Toro and Panama City. My friend Tomás, always alert despite being at the wheel, alerted us: "Have you seen the sky?" We stopped for just five minutes, and the Panamanian sky gifted us this explosion of fire and clouds. A small reminder that on the Pan-American, beauty strikes you at every mile if you know how to stay alert.
