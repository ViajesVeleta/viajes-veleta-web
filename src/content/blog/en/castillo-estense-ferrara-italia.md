During our trip through the Italian **Emilia Romagna**, Ferrara captivated us with its extraordinary monumental heritage. The heart of the city is the **Estense Castle**, a fortress whose construction began in 1385 to defend the powerful Este family from popular insurrections.

Designed by Bartolino da Novara, the castle preserves the typical structure of a medieval fortress with its moat and towers, but from the 16th century onwards, it was transformed into a refined ducal residence. The battlements were replaced with marble balustrades, and vantage points and hanging orange gardens were added. Today, it is possible to visit its ancient kitchens, the historic cells where figures like Parisina Malatesta were imprisoned, and wander through the luxurious halls of the noble floor.
