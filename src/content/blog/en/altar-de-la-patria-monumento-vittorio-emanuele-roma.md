The **Altar of the Fatherland**, officially known as the Victor Emmanuel II National Monument (*Il Vittoriano*), is one of the most imposing and debated structures in **Rome**. Perched between Piazza Venezia and the Capitoline Hill, this white marble colossus was erected to commemorate the unification of Italy and honor its first king.

Designed by Giuseppe Sacconi in the late 19th century, the monument features majestic staircases, Corinthian columns, and spectacular bronze quadrigae crowning its ends. Standing 135 meters wide and over 70 meters high, its immaculate whiteness—sourced from the Botticino quarries—stands in stark contrast to the historical ochre of the Eternal City. The base houses the Museum of Italian Unification and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, making it a pivotal site for understanding modern Italian identity while offering some of the widest panoramic views of central Rome.
