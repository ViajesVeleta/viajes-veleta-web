**Photograph: taken during our group trip three years ago, showing a street in the historic center of Trinidad**

Frozen in time, Cuba remains a different country, with an unequivocally Spanish air despite many French, African, or Jamaican influences. Dreamers, survivors, improvisers, Cubans have made their country a fascinating destination, where beaches are secondary: the best part is the people and their culture. On the southern Caribbean coast, there are essential places like Santiago de Cuba, where drums invoke the 'orishas', Afro-Cuban religious rites are practiced, and folk dances keep slave culture alive. Also on the Caribbean coast, in central Cuba, the sleepy town of Trinidad went to bed in 1850 and never woke up. Today, travelers can explore this perfectly preserved mid-19th-century sugar town, with colonial houses, an accessible countryside, and a great musical atmosphere.
