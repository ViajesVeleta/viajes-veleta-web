**World Heritage Sites**

**Renaissance monumental ensembles of Úbeda and Baeza**

The urban configuration of the two small cities of Úbeda and Baeza, located in the south of Spain, dates from the periods of Moorish rule (9th century) and the Reconquista (13th century). In the 16th century, both cities underwent major changes, as renovation work inspired by the Renaissance style was carried out. These urban transformations were due to the introduction in Spain of humanist ideas coming from Italy and exerted a major influence on the architecture of Latin America.
