Eight kilometers of asphalt, salt spray, and history border the Havana coastline: this is the **Malecón**. More than just an avenue, it is Cuba's great meeting point, where poets, musicians, fishermen, and lovers gather every day to share the city's magic.

Designed in the early 20th century, the Malecón displays an eclectic architecture blending neoclassical and art nouveau styles. During summer nights, the walk transforms with the rhythm of the carnival, where traditional drums merge with salsa and reguetón in an endless party. It is, without a doubt, the best place to witness one of the most beautiful sunsets in the **Caribbean**.
