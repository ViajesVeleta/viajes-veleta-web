**A beautiful city in the north of the province of Guadalajara whose excellent architectural heritage was declared a Historic-Artistic Site in 1965.**

The castle, the cathedral, and the Plaza Mayor are the three must-visit spots in the city, though the streets of Sigüenza are filled with civil and religious buildings of great beauty. The castle is currently a Parador de Turismo. It was built after the Moorish invasion in the 8th century, the era when the alcazaba was constructed. Important remains of the walls are preserved, whose gates and towers start from the castle. The cathedral, begun in 1130, is Romanesque, though it later developed following Gothic canons. Its exterior appearance is that of a medieval fortress with Romanesque towers and portico and an impressive rose window. In the Plaza Mayor or market square, built in the Renaissance style between 1484-1494, the houses of the canons stand out with their balconies and galleries, as well as the Town Hall and the Puerta del Toril.
