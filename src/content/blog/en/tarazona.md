**Tarazona** is the fourth city of Zaragoza. It is located on the slopes of Moncayo and at a crossroads between Aragon, Navarre, Castile and Leon, and La Rioja.

Tarazona is an episcopal and Mudejar town. Its cathedral is one of the most unique of the nine in Aragon, as it adds the Mudejar style of its towers and dome (cimborrio) and its Renaissance facade to the early Gothic. Away from the cathedral is the Episcopal Palace and the Church of the Magdalena. Tarazona also possesses a polygonal bullring from the 18th century formed by houses that are still in use. Tarazona's accommodation and restoration services are highly reputable. As in the rest of Zaragoza, traditional cuisine is characterized by using ingredients without too much manipulation, resulting in sober but tasty dishes. Later, refinements have been added to achieve a more modern cuisine, based on tradition but more imaginative. Notable is the festival of San Atilano on August 27, where a character known as the “Cipotegato” is pelted with tomatoes. Its international photography course is also well-established, and its ancient School of Translators is famous.
