All the great temples of **Siem Reap** feature a canal at their entrance that serves a dual and fascinating function. This image, captured at dawn, reflects the essence of a civilization that knew how to masterfully unite the divine with the practical.

From a spiritual perspective, the canal acts as a mirror of the firmament; by crossing the bridge over its waters, the faithful could imagine they were literally walking on the sky before entering the sacred precinct. However, behind this religious symbolism was a vital hydraulic engineering resource: these canals served to control monsoon flooding and, at the same time, regulate the irrigation of the surrounding lands. As the saying goes: "A Dios rogando y con el mazo dando" (God helps those who help themselves), a philosophy the **Khmer** empire builders applied to perfection to create one of the world's most enduring wonders.
