Only those who have spent an evening on the banks of the Loire can attest to the well-deserved reputation of **Tours** as a young, open, and free city. Known for its *guinguettes* (riverside restaurants) where music and joy are contagious at sunset, this city is pure vitality in the heart of France.

We're talking about a university city with an impressive historical weight, the birthplace of **Honoré de Balzac** and former capital of France under the Bourbons. Its bohemian district of **Saint Martin**, with the squares Du Grand Marché and Plumereau, stands out for its medieval half-timbered houses, while the **Cathedral** district is presided over by one of the most beautiful Gothic temples in France. Tours is a display of religious and civil power, where Jean Nouvel's modern architecture stares face-to-face at the medieval jewels of the past.
