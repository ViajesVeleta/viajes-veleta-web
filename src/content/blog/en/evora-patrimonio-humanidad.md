**Évora** is an art history book in Portuguese form. The best way to visit is on foot, winding through narrow streets with whitewashed houses to discover the monuments and details of Évora's rich legacy.

With roots stretching back to Roman times, the city was the preferred residence of Portuguese kings in the 15th century. Its long history and preserved urban landscape reflecting the 16th to 18th centuries led UNESCO to classify Évora as a World Heritage Site.

**Recommended Itinerary**
Starting from the arches of the **Praça do Giraldo**, visit the Roman temple and baths, the medieval walls, the Cathedral, and the unique **Capela dos Ossos** (Chapel of Bones) in the Church of St. Francis.

Don't miss the Évora Cathedral, the largest medieval cathedral in the country, easily recognized by its conical towers. Nearby stands the grand Roman temple, a symbol of the imperial cult and an emblem of the city.
