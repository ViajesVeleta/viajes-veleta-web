During our journey through the **Rías Altas**, one of the most stunning destinations was **Cape Ortegal**. To reach it, one must travel a narrow and scenic road from the town of Cariño until reaching this point, the second most northern spot on the Iberian Peninsula.

The lighthouse, a cylindrical tower painted in white and red, stands atop spectacular black granitic rocks called "Aguillóns." In this area, the ocean shows its fiercest face, with waves crashing relentlessly against the rocks. It is here that brave **percebeiros** (barnacle hunters) work, extracting what are considered the best barnacles in Galicia from between the clashing waves. The combination of the sea's power, the sheer verticality of the cliffs, and the solitude of the lighthouse makes the visit truly unforgettable.
