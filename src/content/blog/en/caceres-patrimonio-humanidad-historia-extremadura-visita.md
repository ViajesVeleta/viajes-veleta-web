Nearby yet often little-known, the community of **Extremadura** is a land full of attractions that has successfully placed itself on the map of essential destinations. Its crown jewel is undoubtedly the **Old City of Cáceres**, declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site and considered one of the most complete Middle Ages and Renaissance urban ensembles in the world.

## A Walk Through History

Walking through Cáceres is to immerse oneself in a network of streets dotted with Renaissance palaces, Moorish towers, and religious buildings of imposing sobriety.

### The Plaza Mayor and the Wall
The tour usually begins in the **Plaza Mayor**, the city's vital center located outside the medieval walls. From here, you enter the old city through the iconic **Arco de la Estrella**, built in the 18th century to facilitate the passage of carriages. The Wall, of Roman origin but mainly developed during the Almohad era, protected inhabitants from external threats and epidemics, marking the boundary of a world where security was the most precious commodity.

### Corners of Legend
Places like the **Torre de los Púlpitos**, the **Adarve** (the internal bypass lane), and **Santa María Square** offer images of unique stately beauty. In the latter sits the Co-Cathedral of Santa María, from whose bell tower you get spectacular views of the city and the surrounding dehesas (pasturelands). We cannot forget the famous storks, whose "crocoteos" (bill-clattering) and enormous nests on the watchtowers form part of the city's acoustic and visual landscape throughout the year.

### Gastronomy and Welcome
Cáceres doesn't just feed the spirit with its history; its gastronomy based on Iberian pork, Torta del Casar cheeses, and artisanal wines is another of its great pillars. A visit to this Extremaduran city requires time to get lost in its alleys and let oneself be caught by the kindness of its people. Setting aside a couple of days to sleep in its historic quarter, perhaps in an old renovated palace, is the best way to understand how life was in an era as fascinating as the Middle Ages.
