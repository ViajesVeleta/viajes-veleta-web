Known as the **city of shipowners, fishermen, and corsairs**, **Saint-Jean-de-Luz** is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful towns in the French Basque Country. Its fishing port, famous for its tuna and anchovy catches, and its crescent-shaped beach —the *Grande Plage*— enchant everyone who visits.

Strolling through its colorful old town takes you to the Church of Saint John the Baptist and along its iconic promenade, lined with elegant 18th-century houses connected by curious footbridges. Among its architectural gems are the neo-romantic **Grand Hôtel** and the Art Deco casino **La Pergola**.

**A historical fact:** King Louis XIV lived in this city after marrying the Spanish Infanta Maria Theresa, a marriage that ended the war between the two countries.
