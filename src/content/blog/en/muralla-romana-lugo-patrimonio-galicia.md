The Roman walls surrounding the city of **Lugo** are a unique marvel: it is the only fortification from this era preserved in its entirety throughout its entire perimeter. Designated a World Heritage site, its silhouette defies the passing centuries and tells the story of ancient **Lucus Augusti**.

Built more than 17 centuries ago, the wall spans over two kilometers and features 10 gates and 71 towers that still stand today. Walking along its ramparts, which in some sections reach 7 meters in width, allows you to feel the power of Imperial Rome up close while enjoying the best views of the city. Legend has it that it was not built to protect a settlement, but a sacred grove, the mysterious \"Grove of Augustus.\"
