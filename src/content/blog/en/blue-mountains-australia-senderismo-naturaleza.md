The **Blue Mountains** National Park is an essential destination located about 80 km west of Sydney. Declared a **UNESCO** World Heritage Site in 2000, its name comes from the characteristic blue haze produced by oil vapors from its extensive eucalyptus forests.

The park attracts millions of visitors annually with its spectacular gorges, limestone formations, and unique biodiversity that includes rich and ancient plant species. Alongside panoramic trails for hikers, the more adventurous can explore the valleys on mountain bikes following Victorian-era paths. They are a powerful reminder of the majesty of the Australian wilderness and the critical importance of protecting these ecosystems from wildfires.
