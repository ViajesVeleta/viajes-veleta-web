Located over 4,000 meters above sea level in the heart of **Lake Titicaca**, the community of **Taquile Island** maintains a lifestyle where conditions are far from easy but deeply authentic.

In Taquile, traditional clothing is a language in itself. It is one of the few places in the Peruvian Altiplano where women do not wear hats, instead opting for a characteristic black shawl. But perhaps the most curious tradition is the men's code: everyone wears a knitted hat (*chullo*), the color of which indicates their marital status. Married men wear solid red hats, while single men—from young children to elders—wear two-colored hats: white and red. This ingenious system prevents any social confusion, demonstrating how this community has organized its social life through symbols that have withstood the test of centuries amidst the blue waters of the world's highest navigable lake.
