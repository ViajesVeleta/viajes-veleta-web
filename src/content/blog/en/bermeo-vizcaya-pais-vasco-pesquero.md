**Bermeo** is a seafaring village whose port maintains all the traditional fishing character of the Cantabrian coast. Founded in 1239, the villa boasts a rich history reflected in its many monuments declared of historical-artistic interest.

Outstanding among its treasures are the **Ercilla Tower**, a Gothic building that now houses the Fisherman's Museum, and the church of **Santa Eufemia**, known for being the place where the Lords of Biscay swore to the local laws (*fueros*). The old quarter is a maze of charming streets with traditional fishermen's houses bordering the old port. Its lively street life and excellent restaurants, where you can taste the finest fresh fish, are reason enough to lose yourself in this gem of Biscay.
