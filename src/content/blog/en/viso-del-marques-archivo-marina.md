It is fascinating that the great archive of the **Spanish Navy** is located so far from the sea, in the heart of the dry lands of La Mancha. In **Viso del Marqués**, at the foot of Sierra Morena, stands the palace of **Álvaro de Bazán**, the admiral of the Spanish Armada who chose this strategic point for being halfway between the court and the naval bases.

The **Palace of the Marquis of Santa Cruz** is not only an Italian-inspired gem decorated by select Renaissance artists; today it houses more than 80,000 files containing centuries of naval history. As a curious detail, don't miss the town's church, where a Nile crocodile hangs, brought by the Marquis from one of his expeditions.
