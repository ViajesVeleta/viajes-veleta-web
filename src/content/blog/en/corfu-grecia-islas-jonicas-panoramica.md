**Corfu**, the Homeric island of the Phaeacians, was the final stage of Odysseus' legendary journey back to Ithaca. Today, it remains one of the most valued destinations in the Mediterranean, thanks to its mild climate, crystal-clear waters, and unique cosmopolitan character.

The island's landscape offers wonderful contrasts: while the western coasts are rugged and rocky, the eastern side gently meets a transparent Ionian Sea that reflects the deep green of its vegetation. But Corfu is more than just beaches; its capital stands out for its architectural elegance and a vibrant tradition in arts and literature that sets it apart from other Greek islands. Wandering through its picturesque villages is to immerse oneself in a history that has perfectly balanced tradition with modern tourism.
