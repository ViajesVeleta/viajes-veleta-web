With an immense surface area and a relatively small population, much of **Australia** remains practically unpopulated, its territories unchanged since ancient times. This lack of industry and pollution grants travelers and photographers an atmosphere of extraordinary purity, ideal for capturing light in all its splendor.

Traveling along its infinite roads, it's easy to encounter landscapes that force you to pull over. This week's photograph was taken at sunset during our drive from Canberra to Melbourne. It shows a horizon pregnant with **yellows and oranges**, a sky that seemed to burn with overwhelming intensity. It is in these moments, facing the vastness of the Australian *Outback*, that one truly understands the scale and wild beauty of this continent.
