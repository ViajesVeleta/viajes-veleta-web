One of the most iconic images of **Sri Lanka** is its stilt fishermen. Between the towns of Unawatuna and Weligama, it is common to see these men watching the horizon from four-meter poles driven into the coral reefs, waiting patiently with their harpoon.

Interestingly, this tradition is not ancient; it dates back to World War II, when food shortages led local families to use ship remains to gain height and fish for species like mackerel. After the 2004 tsunami, the practice almost disappeared, but today it resurfaces as a mix of necessity and tourist appeal on beaches like **Mirissa**. Contemplating this scene at sunset is one of the most curious experiences in Southeast Asia.
