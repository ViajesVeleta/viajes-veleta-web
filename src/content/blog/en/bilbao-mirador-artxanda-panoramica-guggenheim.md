To understand **Bilbao's** geography, you must go up. This panorama, taken from the **Artxanda Viewpoint**, reveals one of the city's most famous peculiarities: its location in a "botxo" (hole), a valley surrounded by mountains that has shaped its urban development.

From this privileged balcony, you can witness the city's metamorphosis. In the foreground, the titanium of the Guggenheim Museum stands out, with the estuary snaking beside it and the modern Iberdrola Tower watching over the horizon. Taking the historic Artxanda funicular is also the most authentic and recommended way to reach this peak and enjoy what are undoubtedly the best views of the Biscayan capital. An essential perspective for any visitor.
