Human history can only be explained as a **long journey** that began in Africa millions of years ago. Of all our species' feats, perhaps the strangest and most challenging was the arrival of modern man in **Australia**, a continent that has always been an island. About 60,000 years ago, a group of *Homo sapiens* decided to push forward into the unknown, crossing seas without compasses or known maps to inhabit a virgin land.

It is surprising that humans reached Australia 20,000 years before Europe, despite Europe's proximity to Africa. This week, *El Viajero* magazine reflects on how travel defines us as humans. Only a global pandemic momentarily stopped this endless journey, but now it is time to start again and rediscover horizons like those of **Kakadu National Park**, where nature and history embrace under the Australian sun.
