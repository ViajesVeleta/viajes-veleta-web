**Phayao Lake**

With an attractive lakeside setting and easy access to **Chiang Mai** and **Chiang Rai**, the city of **Phayao** is an inviting place to spend a day or two. The surrounding areas are characterized by hills and valleys, and while **Phayao** is not packed with tourist attractions, it is a pleasant and relaxing spot to visit.

The main attraction of **Phayao** is **Kwan Phayao**, the largest freshwater lake in Northern Thailand. This picturesque expanse of water is the hub for local festivals like *Loy Krathong* and significant Buddhist holidays. Beneath the lake lie the remains of an ancient temple, **Wat Tilok Aram**, built in the late 14th century, long before the lake was even created. Atop the temple site, there is now a small island featuring a shrine and a Buddha statue. Boats ferry monks and devotees to the island to pay their respects. A pleasant walk stretches along the south shore of the lake, with several restaurants and cafes where you can sit and enjoy the views.
