The **Canal de Castilla** was the greatest civil engineering project of Enlightenment Spain. Born in the Age of Enlightenment, it sought to link the Castilian capitals with the port of Santander through a navigable network for the transport of grain. Although the work remained unfinished, it left us with 207 kilometers of channel that flow between masonry bridges, 49 locks, and ancient flour mills.

Navigating the canal is to navigate through history. The towpaths, where oxen once dragged the barges, are today perfect routes for hiking and cycling. During our group trip to the province of **Palencia**, we were able to see first-hand this peaceful channel that reflects the dream of a Spain that wanted to open up to the sea from the heart of the plateau.
