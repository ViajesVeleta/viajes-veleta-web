Rome is a city of silent presences guarding the passage of time. Before the imposing **Castel Sant'Angelo**, the angel statues on the Ponte Sant'Angelo act as a celestial guard welcoming the traveler.

This figure of a **protecting angel**, carved with a delicacy that defies the hardness of the stone, is part of the Baroque ensemble designed under Bernini's supervision. Each angel on the bridge carries one of the instruments of the Passion, creating an atmosphere of spirituality and drama that blends with the history of the castle—originally Hadrian's mausoleum. Walking among these marble wings as the sun sets over the Tiber is one of the most evocative experiences the Eternal City can offer.
