**Toledo**, the City of Three Cultures, finds its most recognizable landmark in the **Alcazar** dominating its jagged skyline. This fortification, perched on the highest point of the city, has served as a Roman palace, a medieval fortress, and a royal residence; today, it houses the Spanish Army Museum.

One of the best ways to appreciate the scale of this building and Toledo's unique geography is to distance oneself from the city center bustle and cross the Tagus River to **Los Cigarrales**. From these old country estates, you get a privileged panoramic view where the Alcazar emerges like an eternal sentinel guarding Spanish history. Watching the sunset light bathe its stone walls as the city lights up is a moment that stays etched in every traveler's memory.
