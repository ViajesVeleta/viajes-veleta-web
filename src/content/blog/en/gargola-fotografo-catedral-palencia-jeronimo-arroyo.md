The province of **Palencia** is a treasure trove of Romanesque art, but its cathedral hides a peculiarity that surprises anyone who looks up at its apse: a gargoyle representing the figure of a **photographer**.

This element is not a medieval whim but a personal contribution from **Jerónimo Arroyo**, the architect who led the restoration of the temple in the early 20th century. With this gesture, Arroyo sought to pay an eternal tribute to **José Sanabria**, a popular local figure of the time who dedicated his life to photographing and documenting Palencia's rich artistic heritage. It is a wonderful example of how architecture can integrate contemporary homages into thousand-year-old structures, linking the city's history with the lives of those who worked to capture it for posterity.
