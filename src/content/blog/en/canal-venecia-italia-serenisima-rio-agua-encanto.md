While the Grand Canal is the city's main artery, the true magic of **Venice** lies in its secondary canals. It is in these narrow "rii" that the Serenissima reveals its most intimate and melancholic side, far from the large crowds that usually pack St. Mark's Square.

This image captures a typical corner where facades worn by time and salt reflect in the still, greenish waters. Here, the sound of vaporetto engines is replaced by the soft splashing of water against walls and the distant murmur of daily life. Traversing these canals, whether by gondola or simply strolling across their bridges, is to immerse oneself in a setting that has inspired artists and poets for centuries. Venice is not just a destination; it's a state of mind best discovered when one decides to get lost in its watery labyrinth.
