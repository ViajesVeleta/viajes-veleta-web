During our journey through **Emilia-Romagna**, we discovered that beyond Bologna lie cities with extraordinary heritage, and **Ferrara** is undoubtedly one of them. Its great icon is the **Castello Estense**, an imposing structure surrounded by water that dominates the city center.

Ordered built in 1385 by Niccolò II d'Este to defend the family against popular uprisings, the castle preserves the sturdiness of a medieval fortress combined with the refinement of the 16th-century ducal court. Its marble balconies, towers with viewpoints, and grand interior halls reflect the splendor of the House of Este. Exploring it, visitors can see everything from the dark cells and prisons on the ground floor to the bright hanging garden of orange trees—a peaceful haven with views over this World Heritage city.
