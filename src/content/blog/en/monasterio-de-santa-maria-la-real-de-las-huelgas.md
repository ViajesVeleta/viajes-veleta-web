Founded by King Alfonso VIII and Queen Eleanor of England in 1187, the **Monastery of Santa María la Real de las Huelgas** is one of the most important monastic complexes in Spain. Located in Burgos, it served not only as a spiritual center but also as a royal pantheon and a stage for historic events.

The founding monarchs built a monastery with a Mudejar church, partially preserved in the current Chapel of the Assumption, featuring Almohad style. This contrasts with the Romanesque cloister known as "Las Claustrillas." It consists of 12 arches per side supported by twin columns, typical of this style, with capitals featuring vegetal motifs and small castles.

It was during the first third of the 13th century that the final Gothic monastery was constructed, following the sober architecture characteristic of the Cistercian order. It features a large church with reinforced walls, a cloister, and surrounding rooms, among which the Chapter House stands out.
