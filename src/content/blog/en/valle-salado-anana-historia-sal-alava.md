In the **Basque Country**, specifically in Álava, there is a humanized landscape unique in the world for its unusual architecture: the **Salt Valley of Añana**. This salt exploitation is not a product of modernity; its industrial activity began no less than **seven thousand years ago**.

What makes this valley spectacular is its ingenious system of wooden pipes —over four kilometers— that carry saltwater from natural springs to thousands of stepped terraces. Built by hand using stone, wood, and clay, these \"eras\" (salt pans) are where the sun and wind work the miracle of crystallization. Visiting Añana is witnessing a millennia-old craft that today produces gourmet-quality salt recognized internationally.
