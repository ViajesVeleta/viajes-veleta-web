**Thailand**
**10 immersive experiences to fall in love with Thailand**

This week I leave you with an extensive article from the newspaper La Vanguardia in which it describes different experiences to get to know the country in depth.

In 2019, nearly 40 million travelers from other countries landed at Thai airports. Thailand is one of the ten most visited destinations in the world. And it is curious because it is the only country in Southeast Asia that has not been colonized by a European nation. And the tolerant and dialogue-oriented character of the inhabitants must have something to do with that.

![Ayutthaya, Thailand](assets/asia/tailandia/ayutthaya/10-experiencias-inmersivas-para-enamorarse-de-tailandia)

Thailand, in short, is a place that adapts to the needs of the traveler, lets itself be loved, and gives you what, deep down, you desire. We verified this on a trip from Chiang Rai in the north to the paradise islands in the south.

1. Adventure among tribes in Chiang Rai
Thailand's adventurous capacity is mythical. Geographically its territory is divided into three: the more mountainous north, a flat central area, and a south where beaches rule. 

2. Massages in Chiang Mai
A massage is always welcome, wherever it is. But in Thailand even more so. The traditional Thai massage (nuat thai) is an ancient full-body treatment.

3. The historic city of Sukhothai
To get to know other people in depth it is important to understand their past, and the same goes for places. Thailand has a fascinating history. 

4. The northeast: the village of Ban Chiang
We go from history to prehistory. The northeast of Thailand is possibly the least visited territory by tourists. However, 47 kilometers from Udon Thani is Ban Chiang, one of the most relevant Bronze Age excavations.

5. The route of Buddhist temples
95% of Thailand's population is Buddhist, and it is estimated that there are more than 40,000 temples in the country.

6. Street food and floating markets
One of the great attractions of traveling to Thailand is its food. The stomach is one of the big beneficiaries.

7. The full moon beach party
Thousands of young people flock to Haad Rin beach on the island of Kho Pha Ngan to enjoy drinks, jugglers, fireworks, DJs, and rock music at the Full Moon Party.

8. Diving in the Similan Islands
In Thailand, not only the surface is beautiful, but also the world underwater.

9. Muay Thai, Thai boxing
Thai boxing, or Muay Thai, is a martial tradition widely spread throughout Thailand.

10. Island hopping
It is hard to choose an island in Thailand. There are over 500.
