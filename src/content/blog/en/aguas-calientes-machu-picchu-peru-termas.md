The Peruvian town of **Aguas Calientes** (Matchu Picchu Pueblo) is the essential gateway and the usual resting place for everyone dreaming of visiting the **Machu Picchu** Inca citadel. This town, nestled in a deep valley surrounded by misty mountains, was born in 1911 with the construction of the railway connecting Cusco and Santa María.

Having started as a simple camp for railway workers, it has transformed into a bustling town that pulses with the rhythm of travelers. Its name is no accident; it refers to the thermal springs located just a few hundred meters from the town center, which have historically been credited with miraculous therapeutic properties. Soaking in these waters at the end of a long journey is the perfect prelude before ascending to the world wonder that guards the area from above.
