This week I leave you with the very brief description that the Junta de Castilla y León makes of this small town on its tourism portal.

Perched on a rock that breaks the flow of the Milanos River stands the beautiful village of Calatañazor, which still preserves all the flavor of the Middle Ages in its cobbled streets and squares. Known for the legend that Almanzor lost his drum in this place, this picturesque town seems frozen in time, offering the traveler an atmosphere of authenticity that is hard to match.

A peaceful village rich in architectural heritage, surrounded by lush nature dominated by juniper trees and with traditional Castilian gastronomy featuring dishes like "migas pastoriles", grilled meats, and of course, "torreznos", which will remain in your memory.
