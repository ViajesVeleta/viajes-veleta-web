This week I leave you with the brief description that the **Junta de Castilla y León** gives of this small village on its tourism portal.

Perched on a rocky outcrop that breaks the flow of the **Milanos River** stands the beautiful village of **Calatañazor**, which still preserves all the flavour of the **Middle Ages** in its cobblestone streets and squares. Known for the legend that it was here that *Almanzor lost his drum*, this picturesque village seems frozen in time, offering the traveller an atmosphere of authenticity that is hard to match. A quiet village rich in **architectural heritage**, surrounded by lush nature dominated by **junipers**, with a **traditional Castilian cuisine** featuring dishes such as *migas pastoriles*, grilled meats and, of course, **torreznos** — flavours that will stay with you long after you leave. What are you waiting for?
