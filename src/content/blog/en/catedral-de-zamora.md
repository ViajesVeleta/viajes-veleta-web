**Zamora Cathedral**

**Romanesque**

Some say that Romanesque is coarse, somewhat primitive, and not very spectacular. All these blasphemers have a textbook "shut down" in this building. Zamora has insisted on making what is apparently rough a delight. There are all its contemporary buildings, of a very fine Castilian brutalism, as an example that it is a constant feature of the city. But returning to its main temple, what fascinates about its construction are the details of its lantern tower and its scaled dome, a beautiful, harmonious, and identifiable element with which it manages to demonstrate that, although the city is plagued with contemporary churches and hermitages, none is prettier than this one. **Viajes National Geographic.**
