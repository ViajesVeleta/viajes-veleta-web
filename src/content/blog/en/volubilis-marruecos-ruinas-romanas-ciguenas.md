**Volubilis** is the crown jewel of Roman archaeology in Morocco. Declared a **UNESCO** World Heritage site in 1997, these ruins testify to the splendor reached by the Roman Empire in North Africa. The city was renowned for its wheat production and home to over 20,000 people.

One of the most curious and persistent sights in Volubilis today is the presence of **storks**. Their nests crown ancient capitals and columns, creating a fascinating contrast between modern wildlife and stones that have withstood centuries of history. Since the city's abandonment in the 18th century and the scavenging of its buildings to beautify Meknes, nature has reclaimed this space, where the silence of the ruins is only broken by the clattering of these guardian birds.
