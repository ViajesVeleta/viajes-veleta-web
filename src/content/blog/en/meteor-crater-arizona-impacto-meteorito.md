Crossing the state of Arizona along the legendary **Route 66**, the desert landscape is interrupted by one of Earth's most impressive scars: **Meteor Crater** (also known as Barringer Crater). With a diameter of 1.2 kilometers and a depth of 170 meters, it is the first crater identified by science as the result of an extraterrestrial impact.

It was formed about 50,000 years ago when a 50-meter-wide meteorite struck the ground at the dizzying speed of 45,000 kilometers per hour. Today, walking along its rim and observing the vastness of the impact is an awe-inspiring experience. At the visitor center, a life-sized astronaut replica serves to give perspective on the collosal dimensions of this place that feels otherworldly.
