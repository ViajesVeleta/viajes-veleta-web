The history of **Muxia** is closely linked to the **Sanctuary of A Barca**, a place of magical-religious worship that has fascinated pilgrims for centuries. According to legend, the Virgin arrived here in a stone boat to encourage the Apostle James.

The setting is spectacular: a temple facing the bravery of the Atlantic, surrounded by the famous magic stones with healing properties. Highlights include the *Pedra de Abalar* (rocking stone) and the *Pedra de Os Cadrís* (kidney stone), symbolic remains of that Marian vessel. The "Romeria de la Barca" in September is one of the most authentic and crowded celebrations in Galicia.
