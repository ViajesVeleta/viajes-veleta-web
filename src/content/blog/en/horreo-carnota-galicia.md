In the mythical **Costa da Morte**, between Malpica de Bergantiños and Cape Fisterra, lies one of the most genuine and wild landscapes in Spain. In this legendary stretch of coastline, between Carnota and Lira, we discover an astonishing concentration of hórreos (granaries).

The **Carnota Hórreo**, recognized as a national monument, was built in 1768 and is one of the best-preserved. At 34.74 meters long and supported by 22 pairs of feet, it forms a beautiful architectural complex alongside the dovecote and the Church of Santa Comba. It is said to be one of the most beautiful in Galicia due to its size and spectacular natural setting.
