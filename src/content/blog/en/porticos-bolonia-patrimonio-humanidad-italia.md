The capital of **Emilia-Romagna** hides a unique architectural treasure: its 40 kilometers of **porticoes**, considered the longest in the world. Integrated into the heart of its medieval old town, these walkways were born in the late Middle Ages to gain space on upper floors as the population grew, driven by the prestigious University of Bologna.

Originally built of wood —some of which are still preserved— the porticoes evolved into brick and stone, becoming the city's symbol. Standing out among them is the **Portico of San Luca**, an imposing structure of 3,500 meters and 666 arches that links the city with the sanctuary atop the hill. A marvel recently declared a World Heritage Site.
