Today I leave you with information about one of the cities visited on our February trip and an essential destination in Extremadura: **Plasencia**

**Photographs:** taken during the city tour.

**Text:** extracted from the city's tourism website.

The city of Plasencia is probably one of the best and largest surprises you can find on the peninsula. There are a thousand corners, monuments, and sites to see in Plasencia and its surroundings. Our city is also known as the Pearl of the North or the Pearl of the Valley. It is considered the capital of northern Extremadura and is located on the banks of the Jerte River. Plasencia was founded in the 12th century by Alfonso VIII of Castile, with the dual objective of continuing the Reconquista towards the north of the peninsula and repopulating the conquered territories. At its foundation, Alfonso gave the city its name, which comes from the motto that appeared on the shield: \"Ut placeat Deo et hominibus\" (to please God and men).

Our city is a very popular tourist destination for its welcoming medieval atmosphere. It also stands out for its history, monuments, and for an ideal strategic situation to explore the northern regions.

![A beautiful church in Plasencia](assets/europa/espana/plasencia/plasencia-iglesia-1)

**What to see in Plasencia in a morning:**

The light is ideal for seeing the cathedrals. Both are joined together, which is why the "old" and "new" cathedrals are also known as the hybrid cathedral of Plasencia. On its facade, you can see all kinds of details, shields, and much of the city's Christian culture. The new cathedral also houses a majestic altarpiece and the stalls in the Choir of the Catholic Monarchs.

Near Plasencia's cathedrals is the Episcopal Palace—a building over 800 years old that has hosted popes, bishops, and great personalities of the time.

Another must-visit in Plasencia during the day is the Palace of the Marquis of Mirabel. Its exterior still preserves the splendor of the Spanish grandees of the time, which has recently been revitalized by hosting the wedding of Álvaro Falcó and Isabelle Junot. The Mirabel Palace is attached to the church and convent of Santo Domingo (now a National Parador), in San Nicolás Square.

**What to see in Plasencia in an afternoon:**

To see Plasencia in an afternoon, it is essential to start at the Plaza Mayor. Here you can see the Municipal Palace, currently the Town Hall, with Grandfather Mayorga at the top marking the passing hours.

![The Plaza Mayor in the afternoon](assets/europa/espana/plasencia/plasencia-iglesia-3)

Near the Plaza Mayor, we find some of the most important manor houses of the period, such as the Grijalva / Almaraz House, the Dean's and Doctor Trujillo's House, or the Monroy Palace "House of the Two Towers."

Another essential site to visit is the Plasencia Parador / Convent of San Vicente Ferrer, a building with an immense historical legacy that is currently the second largest in the city, but which in the past was the temple of Plasencia's Jewish quarter.

This Sephardic past is reflected in the streets as a tribute to the strong and prosperous Jewish culture that coexisted in our city until the 15th century.

The ideal way to see Plasencia in an afternoon is to end the visit at sunset, so that the lighting of the wall and Lucia Tower allows you to enjoy the magic of this city.
