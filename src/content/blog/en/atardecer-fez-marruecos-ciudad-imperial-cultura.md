Famous for its labyrinthine walled city, which for many evokes the essence of ancient Jerusalem, **Fez** stands as the first of Morocco's imperial cities. Its origins date back to the 8th century, and it has since consolidated its position as the cultural and spiritual capital of the kingdom.

Walking through Fez means immersing yourself in a center of art and science that houses one of the world's oldest universities still in operation. As sunset arrives, the golden light bathes its walls and minarets, granting the city an atmosphere of mystery and nobility. It is at this moment that one best understands why it is considered the epicenter of Muslim worship in the country and a pioneer in preserving a living, influential culture that continues to fascinate everyone who chooses to lose themselves in its streets.
