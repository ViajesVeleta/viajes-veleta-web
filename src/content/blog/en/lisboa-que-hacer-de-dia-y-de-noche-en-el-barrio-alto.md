**Lisbon and Barrio Alto**

This week I leave you with an article read in **La Vanguardia**. It describes activities to do in Lisbon's Barrio Alto. Below the photograph, you'll find the link to the full text.

*Barrio Alto is just the beginning of a journey through the senses in the city of Lisbon. There are viewpoints waiting in Alfama, literary cafes where the poet Fernando Pessoa used to spend his days reading, and a Belém Tower pointing to the past. And if you get lost, nostalgia will guide you—the saudade brought by the ships. Trams that seem to play with time and return you to some lost place in memory.*

![A typical street in Lisbon's Barrio Alto](assets/europa/espana/todo/lisboa-que-hacer-de-dia-y-de-noche-en-el-barrio-alto-1)

**All roads in the Portuguese capital lead to Barrio Alto, the icon where you can lose yourself among narrow streets with hanging laundry, historic trams, and a nightlife that bursts into unique bars and restaurants.**
