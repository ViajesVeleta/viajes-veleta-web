In the state of Queensland, in northeastern Australia, lies one of the most spectacular spots on the planet: **Cape Tribulation**. This natural stage possesses a privileged location where two UNESCO World Heritage sites converge: the **Daintree Tropical Rainforest**—one of the world's oldest, dating back over 135 million years—and the **Great Barrier Reef**.

It is a unique territory where time seems to have stood still for eons. Located about 110 kilometers north of Cairns, Cape Tribulation offers untouched nature composed of lush tropical jungles that meet pristine sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters. It is a place where you can walk along trails shrouded in prehistoric vegetation and, just a few meters away, dive in to explore Earth's most vibrant marine ecosystem. Without exaggeration, Cape Tribulation is the perfect definition of a "Paradise on Earth," a sanctuary where biodiversity reaches its maximum expression.
