The Mayan temples of **Tikal** rise majestically in the middle of the Petén jungle, in northern Guatemala. This archaeological complex represents the cultural and artistic peak of a civilization that occupied the city for more than 1,600 years.

Walking through Tikal is feeling like a true explorer. Among vines and the sound of spider monkeys, the **Great Plaza**, the imposing **Temple IV** —the second tallest pre-Columbian building in the Americas— and the Lost World Pyramid appear. Climbing to their crests allows you to overlook an infinite green sea that hides relics of a glorious past. Tikal is not just an open-air museum; it is a spiritual journey to the heart of Mayan culture.
