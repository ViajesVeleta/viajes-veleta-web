Traveling **Route 66** from Chicago to Los Angeles has many mandatory stops, but none are as anticipated as the **Grand Canyon of the Colorado River**. We had been warned: "You are going to see one of the most beautiful places on the planet."

So, the day before, we meticulously checked our equipment, charged batteries, and cleaned lenses. Once at the National Park entrance, my friend Tomás—always attentive to detail—asked the park ranger at the ticket booth for the ideal overlook to capture the sunset. With an "enjoy your trip," he pointed to a spot on the map we headed to without hesitation. This week's photo is the result of that advice. When the sun begins to drop and the long shadows play across the canyon's geological layers, you realize that reality surpasses any postcard. The ranger was right: he wasn't off track at all.
