A quarter of a century ago, **Bilbao** showed the world it had a bright future beyond its industrial past. The closing of the shipyards in 1988 marked a turning point that found its direction with the opening of the **Guggenheim Museum**.

Today, the museum is not only the most visited place in Euskadi but also the driver of a transformation that has changed the color of the city and its estuary. The Bilbao Estuary is now an example of how culture and architecture can regenerate an urban space, turning it into a modern, vibrant, and sustainable destination.
