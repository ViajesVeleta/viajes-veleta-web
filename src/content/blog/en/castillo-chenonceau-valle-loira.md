The **Chenonceau Castle**, located in the Loire Valley region, is a masterpiece of French Renaissance architecture. Owned by the Crown and a former royal residence, it is currently the most visited castle in France after Versailles.

Known as the **\"Ladies' Castle\"**, its history has been shaped by influential women such as Katherine Briçonnet, Diane de Poitiers, and Catherine de' Medici. Its unique design, built literally across the Cher River, makes it an exceptional site. In addition to its exterior beauty, the castle houses an impressive collection of paintings by masters such as Murillo, Tintoretto, and Rubens, along with notable 16th-century Flemish tapestries.
