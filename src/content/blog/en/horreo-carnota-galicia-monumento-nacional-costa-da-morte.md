Along the legendary **Costa da Morte** (Coast of Death), between Malpica de Bergantiños and Cape Fisterra, lies one of Spain's most authentic areas. Nestled among sheer cliffs and fishing villages, in the town of **Carnota**, we find a masterpiece of Galician folk architecture: its celebrated *hórreo* (granary).

Recognized as a National Monument, the **Carnota Granary** was built in 1768 and is one of the best-preserved in the region. At an impressive 34.74 meters long and supported by 22 pairs of pillars, this "stone giant" is part of an exceptional architectural ensemble alongside a manor house, a dovecote, and the Church of Santa Comba. Its design, filled with ornamental details, once served to protect crops, but today it stands as a symbol of agricultural power and the beauty of tradition along this legendary coast where land and sea wage constant battle.
