On the mythical **Costa da Morte** (Coast of Death), among cliffs and legends of shipwrecks, we discover one of Galicia's most genuine structures: the **Carnota Hórreo** (granary). Declared a National Monument, this stone giant built in 1768 measures nearly 35 meters long and stands on 22 pairs of feet.

Located next to a magnificent manor house, a dovecote, and the church of Santa Comba, the granary stands out for its elegance and ornamental details. It is a symbol of the area's agricultural wealth and a must-stop to admire how Galician popular architecture reaches its maximum expression in front of the ferocity of the Atlantic.
