Sometimes, the most boring part of a trip—bureaucratic airport procedures—can gift us memorable moments for the most unexpected reasons. We were in Cambodia, filling out the entry form after landing on a **Cambodia Angkor Air** flight.

My travel companion, Jorge, got stuck on one of the form's questions. "What do I put in the Sex section?" he asked me. "Sometimes," I replied wryly. What I didn't expect was for Jorge to naturally write exactly that. Minutes later, in front of the police officer, a surreal dialogue ensued. When the officer pointed out the answer was "wrong," Jorge insisted that I—his expert companion—had told him it was correct. The policeman, after looking at us with a grin, eventually accepted the form with a smile. In the end, in this Asian paradise, perhaps we were all a little bit right!
