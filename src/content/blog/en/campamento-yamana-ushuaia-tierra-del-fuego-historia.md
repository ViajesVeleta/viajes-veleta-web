The **Yamana** (or Yaghan) communities have populated the southernmost areas of Chile and Argentina since immemorial times, facing one of the planet's harshest and most extreme climates. This image, captured at a reconstruction near **Ushuaia**, shows what a typical camp of this nomadic sea community was like.

Their conical huts, built with branches and skins, were their refuge against the frigid winds of the Beagle Channel. Unfortunately, contact with northern colonizers and the arrival of unknown diseases spelled the end for their rich culture, which is now practically extinct. Preserving these types of spaces is essential to remember the resilience and amazing adaptation of a people who made the "end of the world" their eternal home.
