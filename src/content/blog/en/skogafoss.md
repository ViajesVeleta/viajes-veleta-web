This week I leave you with one of the waterfalls we will visit on our July trip to Iceland: **Skógafoss.**

*On the final stretch of the Skógá River lies Skógafoss, probably the most famous waterfall in Iceland and one of the most beautiful, often referred to as the “perfect waterfall.”*

*Skógafoss stands 62 meters high and almost 30 meters wide. It is located in an impressive spot: the water falls onto a bed of black stones that contrast with the emerald green of the hillsides surrounding the falls. This contrast is completely different in winter, when snow covers everything in a wonderful white mantle.*

*The Skógafoss waterfall can be admired from below, where the curtain of water forms a spectacular rainbow on sunny days, as well as from the very top. All those who dare to climb the more than 400 steps will enjoy spectacular views of Iceland's south coast and can perfectly observe from a metal balcony how the water thunders furiously and incessantly down the Skógafoss waterfall.*
