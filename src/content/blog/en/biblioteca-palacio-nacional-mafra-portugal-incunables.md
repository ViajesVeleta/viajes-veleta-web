Today, April 23rd, we celebrate World Book Day, and there is no better occasion to share with you a photograph of one of the world's most beautiful and extraordinary libraries: the **Library of the National Palace of Mafra** in Portugal.

Beyond the impressive architectural beauty shown in this image, with its finely carved wooden bookshelves and solemn atmosphere, Mafra houses an incalculable treasure for humanity. Its shelves guard nearly 40,000 volumes, including several incunables—books published during the 15th century, at the dawn of the printing press—of exceptional value. Entering this space is to feel the weight of centuries and the veneration for knowledge that has guided our civilization. A sanctuary for books in one of our neighboring country's monumental jewels.
