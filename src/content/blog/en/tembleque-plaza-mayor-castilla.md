About 60 kilometers from Toledo lies **Tembleque**, a town in La Mancha that holds one of the most unique squares in Spain. Declared a Site of Cultural Interest, its **Plaza Mayor** is a fascinating example of Castilian architecture, inspired by the old **corrales de comedias** (open-air theaters).

Square-shaped and arcaded with granite columns, the square stands out for its upper wooden corridors and whitewashed facades. It was conceived not only as a central hub but as a stage for bullfighting and festive shows. Under its arcades, it is a delight to enjoy La Mancha's gastronomy in a setting that seems frozen in the time of the Golden Age.
