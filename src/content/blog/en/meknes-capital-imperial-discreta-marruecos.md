At the foot of the Middle Atlas mountains stands **Meknes**, the most discreet but no less beautiful of **Morocco's** imperial capitals. Its smaller size compared to Fez or Marrakech is, in fact, its greatest virtue: it allows for a thorough exploration without the typical tourist crowds found in other regions.

Designated a World Heritage site, Meknes reached its splendor under the reign of Sultan Moulay Ismail in the late 17th century. The sultan dedicated decades to building 40 kilometers of walls, immense granaries, and monumental gates such as **Bab Mansour**. Wandering through its medina is to immerse yourself in the history of a city that, despite its discretion, has nothing to envy of its larger sisters.
