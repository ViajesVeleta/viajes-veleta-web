I have a friend who classifies trips into "those you should see and **those you can't die without seeing**." **Angkor** undeniably belongs in the second category.

These Cambodian ruins, remains of the great Khmer Empire that ruled from the 9th to 15th centuries, form one of Southeast Asia's most fascinating archaeological sites. It's a place worth visiting at least once in a lifetime.

When standing before temples like **Ta Prohm**, swallowed by massive tree roots, one can easily fall into Stendhal syndrome. Due to its scale, jungle setting, and sculptural quality, Angkor stands alongside the Egyptian pyramids or Machu Picchu.
