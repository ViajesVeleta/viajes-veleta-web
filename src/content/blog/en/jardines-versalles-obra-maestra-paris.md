The **Gardens of Versailles** are considered some of the largest and most magnificent in the world. Built by order of Louis XIV in 1661 and designed by the legendary landscape architect André Le Nôtre, these gardens were deemed as important as the palace itself, taking over 40 years to complete.

The creation of this space was a monumental task that involved reclaiming swamplands, moving vast amounts of earth, and bringing in trees from every corner of France. Characterized by their geometric walks, spectacular fountains, sculptures, and parterres, the gardens are the pinnacle of 17th-century French design. During a recent group trip, we were able to admire the magnitude of this work that continues to amaze every visitor.
