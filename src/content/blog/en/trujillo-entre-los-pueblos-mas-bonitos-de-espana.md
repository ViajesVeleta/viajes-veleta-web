Today I leave you with one of the additions to the list of **"The Most Beautiful Villages in Spain"**. Among the new ones are: Ampudia (Palencia), Comillas (Cantabria), Parauta (Málaga), Trevejo, and Trujillo (Cáceres). With the new additions, it totals 116 locations.

**Trujillo (Cáceres)**

Located 45 kilometers from the city of Cáceres and about two and a half hours by car from Madrid, behind its doors, Visigoth, Roman, Arab, and Christian remains intermingle and merge around its Main Square (in the photo), which is of Renaissance style. It features such symbolic buildings as the Palace of the Marquis of the Conquest, with a detailed balconied corner that catches the eyes of tourists. Or the Carvajal Vargas Palace, as well as the Romanesque church of Santiago (from the 12th century), the church of San Martín, or the equestrian statue in honor of Francisco Pizarro, the conqueror of Peru born in Trujillo in 1478. "All of them claim the history and wealth of this town in Cáceres," argue the representatives of the association. As a curiosity: its castle—located in the highest part of the town and from which magnificent views are obtained—served to film the Game of Thrones sequence in which the Lannister family invades the fortress of Highgarden, and it appears again in the series House of the Dragon. In fact, the equestrian statue of Francisco Pizarro was replaced by that of a dragon. The kid goat frite, the moraga, or the bishop's soup are some of the gastronomic pleasures that await here.
