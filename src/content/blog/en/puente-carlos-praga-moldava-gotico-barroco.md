The **Charles Bridge** is the oldest in **Prague** and crosses the Vltava River, connecting the Old Town with Malá Strana (Lesser Town). Construction began in 1357 under King Charles IV and was completed in 1402, making it the most critical communication route for trade between Eastern and Western Europe for centuries.

Stretching 516 meters and supported by 16 arches, the bridge is an open-air museum. It is adorned with 30 statues, mostly Baroque in style and sculpted around 1700, and protected by three impressive Gothic towers. While at night the bridge remains a silent and mysterious witness to medieval times, by day it transforms into the beating heart of the city, filled with artists, musicians, and travelers from around the world captivated by its majesty.
