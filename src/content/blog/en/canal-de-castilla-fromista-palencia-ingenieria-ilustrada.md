The **Canal de Castilla** is one of the most unique and ambitious projects of 18th-century Spain. Conceived to facilitate the transport of Castilian cereal to the northern ports, this waterway traverses the lands of Palencia, Burgos, and Valladolid, leaving in its wake scenes of serene and nostalgic beauty.

In this image, we capture the canal as it passes through **Frómista**, in the province of Palencia. Here, engineering merges with the plateau landscape, showing the locks and towpaths that once bustled with commercial activity. Today, the Canal de Castilla is a green corridor ideal for hiking and recreational boating, a reminder of the Enlightenment era that sought to transform our country's reality through reason and progress. A corner where the water continues to narrate the story of a dream that challenged the dryness of the Castilian land.
