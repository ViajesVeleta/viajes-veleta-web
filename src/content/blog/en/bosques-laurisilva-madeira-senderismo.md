The **Laurisilva** Forest is one of the most important natural treasures of the **Madeira** archipelago. This unique ecosystem, taking its name from the Latin roots *Laurus* (laurel) and *Silva* (forest), is a subtropical wet forest that has survived since the Tertiary period.

Declared a World Natural Heritage site by **UNESCO** in 1999, it covers approximately 15,000 hectares within the Madeira Natural Park. It formed about 20 million years ago when this type of vegetation covered much of the Mediterranean basin, Southern Europe, and Northern Africa. Today, Madeira preserves one of the largest and best-preserved expanses of this ancient habitat, offering some of the highest botanical diversity per square kilometer in the world. Walking its trails is like a journey through time back to the prehistory of our continent.
